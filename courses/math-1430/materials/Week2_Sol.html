Calculus 2 • Power Series & Radius of Convergence

1. Calculus on Power Series

Find the integral ($\int f(x) dx$) and derivative ($f'(x)$) of each power series.

  1. $ f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n x^{2n} $
    Derivative $f'(x)$:
    Differentiate using the Power Rule $\frac{d}{dx}x^{2n} = 2n x^{2n-1}$. $$ f'(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2n (-1)^n x^{2n-1} $$
    Integral $\int f(x)dx$:
    Integrate using the Power Rule $\int x^{2n} dx = \frac{x^{2n+1}}{2n+1}$. $$ \int f(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{2n+1} $$
  2. $ g(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} n! x^n $
    Derivative $g'(x)$:
    $$ g'(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} n \cdot n! x^{n-1} $$
    Integral $\int g(x)dx$:
    $$ \int g(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{n! x^{n+1}}{n+1} $$
  3. $ h(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{2^n} $
    Derivative $h'(x)$:
    $$ h'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n x^{n-1}}{2^n} $$
    Integral $\int h(x)dx$:
    Increase the exponent to $n+1$ and divide by the new exponent. $$ \int h(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n+1}}{2^n(n+1)} $$
  4. $ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 5^n (x-3)^n $
    Derivative $f'(x)$:
    Apply the chain rule/power rule to $(x-3)^n$. $$ f'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n 5^n (x-3)^{n-1} $$
    Integral $\int f(x)dx$:
    $$ \int f(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{5^n (x-3)^{n+1}}{n+1} $$
  5. $ g(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(x-5)^{2n}}{n!} $
    Derivative $g'(x)$:
    The exponent $2n$ comes down, reducing the new exponent to $2n-1$. $$ g'(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{2n(x-5)^{2n-1}}{n!} $$
    Integral $\int g(x)dx$:
    Add 1 to the exponent ($2n+1$) and divide by that new value. $$ \int g(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(x-5)^{2n+1}}{n! (2n+1)} $$
  6. $ h(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} x^{3n} $
    Derivative $h'(x)$:
    $$ h'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 3n x^{3n-1} $$
    Integral $\int h(x)dx$:
    $$ \int h(x)dx = C + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^{3n+1}}{3n+1} $$

2. New Taylor Series

Find the Taylor Series for each function centered at $x=a$ (using substitution on known series).

  1. $ \sin(x^3), \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: Substitute $x^3$ into the standard sine series $\sum \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$. $$ \sin(x^3) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n (x^3)^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} $$
    Simplify Exponents: $(x^3)^{2n+1} = x^{3(2n+1)} = x^{6n+3}$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{6n+3}}{(2n+1)!} $$
  2. $ \cos(-x), \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: Substitute $-x$ into the cosine series $\sum \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$. $$ \cos(-x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n (-x)^{2n}}{(2n)!} $$
    Simplify: Since the exponent $2n$ is always even, $(-x)^{2n} = x^{2n}$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n)!} $$
  3. $ f(x) = (x-1)^3, \quad a=0 $
    Definition Method: Calculate derivatives at $a=0$.
    • $f(x) = (x-1)^3 \implies f(0) = (-1)^3 = -1$
    • $f'(x) = 3(x-1)^2 \implies f'(0) = 3(-1)^2 = 3$
    • $f''(x) = 6(x-1) \implies f''(0) = 6(-1) = -6$
    • $f'''(x) = 6 \implies f'''(0) = 6$
    • $f^{(4)}(x) = 0 \implies f^{(4)}(0) = 0$ (all subsequent are zero)
    Construct Series: $$ y = -1 + 3(x-0) - \frac{6}{2!}(x-0)^2 + \frac{6}{3!}(x-0)^3 $$ $$ \boxed{y = -1 + 3x - 3x^2 + x^3} $$
  4. $ \frac{1}{1-x^3}, \quad a=0 $
    Geometric Series: Use $\frac{1}{1-u} = \sum u^n$. Let $u = x^3$. $$ \frac{1}{1-x^3} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (x^3)^n $$ $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^{3n} $$
  5. $ e^{x^2}, \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: Substitute $x^2$ into $e^u = \sum \frac{u^n}{n!}$. $$ e^{x^2} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(x^2)^n}{n!} $$ $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{2n}}{n!} $$
  6. $ \frac{1}{1-x/3}, \quad a=0 $
    Geometric Series: Let $u = x/3$. $$ \frac{1}{1-x/3} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{x}{3}\right)^n $$ $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{3^n} $$
  7. $ \frac{\sin(x)}{x}, \quad a=0 $
    Write Series and Divide: $$ \frac{1}{x} \sin(x) = \frac{1}{x} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} $$
    Simplify: Bring $1/x$ inside the sum. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x} \cdot \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} $$ Note: Subtract exponent $(2n+1) - 1 = 2n$ to account for division by $x$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n+1)!} $$
  8. $ \frac{\cos(x)}{x}, \quad a=0 $
    Write Series and Divide: $$ \frac{\cos(x)}{x} = \frac{1}{x} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n)!} $$ Note: Again subtract one from exponent to account for division by $x$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n-1}}{(2n)!} $$
  9. $ x e^x, \quad a=0 $
    Write Series and Multiply: $$ x e^x = x \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x \cdot \frac{x^n}{n!} $$ Note: Add one to exponent when multiplying by $x$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n+1}}{n!} $$
  10. $ \cos(\sqrt{x}), \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: Substitute $\sqrt{x}$ into cosine series. $$ \cos(\sqrt{x}) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n (\sqrt{x})^{2n}}{(2n)!} $$ Simplify $(\sqrt{x})^{2n} = (x^{1/2})^{2n} = x^n$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^n}{(2n)!} $$
  11. $ \sin(\sqrt{x}), \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: Substitute $\sqrt{x}$ into sine series. $$ \sin(\sqrt{x}) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n (\sqrt{x})^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} $$ Simplify exponents: $(\sqrt{x})^{2n+1} = x^{(2n+1)/2} = x^{n + 1/2}$. $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{n+1/2}}{(2n+1)!} $$
  12. $ e^{3x}, \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: $$ e^{3x} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(3x)^n}{n!} $$ $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{3^n x^n}{n!} $$
  13. $ e^{x/2}, \quad a=0 $
    Substitution: $$ e^{x/2} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(x/2)^n}{n!} $$ $$ = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n! 2^n} $$

3. Radius of Convergence

Find the center ($a$) and radius of convergence ($R$) for each power series.
Using the theorem $L = \lim |a_{n+1}/a_n|$ or $L = \lim \sqrt[n]{|a_n|}$ where $R = 1/L$.

  1. $ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2^n x^n $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Root Test on coefficients ($a_n = 2^n$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sqrt[n]{|2^n|} = \lim_{n\to\infty} 2 = 2 $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \boxed{\frac{1}{2}} $$
  2. $ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{3^n} $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Root Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{1}{3^n}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sqrt[n]{\left|\frac{1}{3^n}\right|} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{3} $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{1/3} = \boxed{3} $$
  3. $ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{n x^n}{2^n} $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Ratio Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{n}{2^n}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{n+1}{2^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{2^n}{n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{n+1}{n} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \right| $$ $$ L = (1) \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2} $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{1/2} = \boxed{2} $$
  4. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{e^n} $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Root Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{1}{e^n}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sqrt[n]{\left|\frac{1}{e^n}\right|} = \frac{1}{e} $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{1/e} = \boxed{e} $$
  5. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n x^n}{n!} $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Ratio Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{10^n}{n!}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{10^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} \cdot \frac{n!}{10^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{10}{n+1} = 0 $$ $$ \text{Since } L=0, \text{ Radius } R = \boxed{\infty} $$
  6. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^n}{n!} $
    Center: $a=0$
    Test: Ratio Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{(-1)^n}{n!}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} \cdot \frac{n!}{(-1)^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{-1}{n+1} \right| = 0 $$ $$ \text{Since } L=0, \text{ Radius } R = \boxed{\infty} $$
  7. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 3^n (x-1)^n $
    Center: $a=1$
    Test: Root Test on coefficients ($a_n = 3^n$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sqrt[n]{|3^n|} = 3 $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \boxed{\frac{1}{3}} $$
  8. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n(x-3)^n}{5^n} $
    Center: $a=3$
    Test: Ratio Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{n}{5^n}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{n+1}{5^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{5^n}{n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{n+1}{n} \cdot \frac{1}{5} \right| = \frac{1}{5} $$ $$ \text{Radius } R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{1/5} = \boxed{5} $$
  9. $ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(x-10)^n}{n!} $
    Center: $a=10$
    Test: Ratio Test on coefficients ($a_n = \frac{1}{n!}$) $$ L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{1}{(n+1)!} \cdot \frac{n!}{1} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n+1} = 0 $$ $$ \text{Since } L=0, \text{ Radius } R = \boxed{\infty} $$
