Module 11

Formal Proof Methods for Quantifiers

Formal quantifier rules, restrictions on variables, and structured first-order derivations.

Guiding Questions

  • What does universal introduction look like in a formal proof?
  • What does universal elimination look like in a formal proof?
  • What does existential introduction look like in a formal proof?
  • What does existential elimination look like in a formal proof?
  • What are the Peano Axioms for arithmetic?
  • What does the axiom of extension say about sets?
  • Why is the axiom of comprehension inconsistent?
  • What does the axiom schema of separation say about sets?

Objectives

  • Construct formal proofs in Fitch using introduction and elimination rules for quantifiers.
  • Construct formal proofs in Fitch using the rule of general conditional proof .
  • Apply the axiomatic method : Zermelo-Fraenkel Set Theory ( ZF )
  • Peano Arithmetic ( PA )
  • Shape Axioms
