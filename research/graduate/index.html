Graduate Research

My graduate work marks the path from analysis into logic and infinite combinatorics. The dissertation is where my current research program takes shape. The master's thesis records an earlier part of my training in nonlinear analysis and dynamics.

Doctoral Dissertation

Topological Ramsey spaces, associated ultrafilters and their applications to the Tukey theory of ultrafilters and Dedekind cuts of nonstandard arithmetic. Advisor: Natasha Dobrinen, University of Denver.

This work studies topological Ramsey spaces and the ultrafilters canonically associated with them. The main point is structural: when a space has a Ramsey theorem strong enough to support canonical forms, the corresponding ultrafilters can be compared through Tukey theory, and those comparisons reveal information about maps, reductions, and Dedekind cuts in nonstandard arithmetic.

Master's Thesis

Attractors for nonlinear reaction-diffusion equations in unbounded domains. Advisor: Bixiang Wang, New Mexico Institute of Mining and Technology.

This thesis belongs to my earlier work in analysis. It studies long-term behavior for nonlinear reaction-diffusion equations in unbounded domains, with attention to attractors and the analytic estimates needed to make asymptotic behavior precise.
