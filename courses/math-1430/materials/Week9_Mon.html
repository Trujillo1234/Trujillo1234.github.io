Arc Length and Surface Area

Calculus 2 • Week 9 Monday Notes • 3/16/26
Learning Objectives
  • Find the length of a curve $y=f(x)$ on an interval $[a,b]$.
  • Find the surface area obtained by rotating a curve about the x-axis.
  • Find the surface area obtained by rotating a curve about the y-axis.

1. Arc Length of a Curve

To find the length of a curve $y=f(x)$ on $[a,b]$, we can approximate it by breaking the curve into small straight line segments.

Approximation: $\text{length} \approx s_1 + s_2 + s_3 + s_4 = \sum_{i=1}^n s_i$ (for $n=4$)

Zooming in on a segment $s_i$

The distance $s_i$ between points $(x_{i-1}, f(x_{i-1}))$ and $(x_i, f(x_i))$ forms a right triangle.

$$ (s_i)^2 = (\Delta x)^2 + (\Delta y)^2 $$

Factoring out $(\Delta x)^2$:

$$ (s_i)^2 = \left( 1 + \left(\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\right)^2 \right) (\Delta x)^2 $$ $$ s_i = \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\right)^2} \cdot \Delta x $$

Taking the limit as $n \to \infty$, the sum of these segments becomes our integral formula:

$$ \text{Total length of } f(x) \text{ on } [a,b] = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\right)^2} \cdot \Delta x $$ $$ = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + (f'(x))^2} \,dx $$
Example 1: Find the arc length of the curve.

Find the length of $y = x^{3/2}$ on $[0,1]$.

$$ \text{length} = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + (f'(x))^2} \,dx $$ $$ = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{3}{2}x^{1/2}\right)^2} \,dx $$ $$ = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + \frac{9}{4}x} \,dx $$
U-Substitution
$u = 1 + \frac{9}{4}x$
$du = \frac{9}{4}\,dx \implies \frac{4}{9}\,du = dx$
$u(0) = 1 + \frac{9}{4}(0) = 1$
$u(1) = 1 + \frac{9}{4}(1) = \frac{13}{4}$
$$ = \int_{1}^{13/4} \sqrt{u} \cdot \frac{4}{9} \,du $$ $$ = \frac{4}{9} \int_{1}^{13/4} u^{1/2} \,du $$ $$ = \frac{4}{9} \left[ \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} \right]_{1}^{13/4} $$ $$ = \frac{8}{27} u^{3/2} \Big|_{1}^{13/4} $$ $$ = \frac{8}{27} \left( \frac{13}{4} \right)^{3/2} - \frac{8}{27}(1)^{3/2} \approx 1.4397 $$

2. Surface Area

When we rotate a curve about an axis, we get a surface. A small line segment $s_i$ rotated around an axis creates a narrow band (which is a frustum of a cone).

Curve segments rotated about x-axis
Surface area of $i$-th part
$\text{Surface area} \approx 2\pi f(x_i) \cdot s_i$

Rotation about the x-axis

Using the radius $f(x_i)$ and the slant length $s_i = \sqrt{1+(f'(x_i))^2}\Delta x$, the sum becomes an integral:

$$ \text{Total surface area} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n 2\pi f(x_i) \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\right)^2} \Delta x $$
$$ = \int_a^b 2\pi f(x) \sqrt{1 + (f'(x))^2} \,dx $$

Rotation about the y-axis

What about rotating about the y-axis? The radius of revolution changes to $x$ instead of $f(x)$.

$\text{Surface area from the } i\text{-th part} \approx 2\pi x_i \cdot s_i$
$$ \text{Total surface area about y-axis} = \int_a^b 2\pi x \sqrt{1 + (f'(x))^2} \,dx $$

3. Comprehensive Setup Example

Example 2: Setup integrals for length and surface area.

Find integrals for the length of $y = \ln|\sec(x)+\tan(x)|$ on $[0, \pi/4]$. Then find integrals for the surface area when rotating about the x-axis and y-axis. (Do not evaluate integrals).

First, find the derivative $f'(x)$:

$$ y' = \frac{\sec(x)\tan(x) + \sec^2(x)}{\sec(x)+\tan(x)} $$ $$ y' = \frac{\sec(x)(\tan(x)+\sec(x))}{\sec(x)+\tan(x)} = \sec(x) $$

1) Arc Length Setup:

$$ \text{length} = \int_0^{\pi/4} \sqrt{1 + \sec^2(x)} \,dx $$

2) Surface Area about the x-axis Setup:

$$ \text{SA}_{x} = \int_0^{\pi/4} 2\pi \ln|\sec(x)+\tan(x)| \sqrt{1 + \sec^2(x)} \,dx $$

3) Surface Area about the y-axis Setup:

$$ \text{SA}_{y} = \int_0^{\pi/4} 2\pi x \sqrt{1 + \sec^2(x)} \,dx $$
