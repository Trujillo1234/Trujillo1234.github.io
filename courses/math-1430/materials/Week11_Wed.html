2. Examples with Logarithms

Ex: $\int \ln(x) \, dx =$
$u = \ln(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = dx$
$v = x$
$$ x\ln(x) - \int x \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$ $$ = x\ln(x) - \int dx $$
$$ = x\ln(x) - x + C $$
Ex: $\int \frac{\ln(x)}{x^3} \, dx =$
$u = \ln(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = x^{-3} \, dx$
$v = \frac{x^{-2}}{-2} = \frac{-1}{2x^2}$
$$ -\frac{1}{2x^2}\ln(x) + \int \frac{1}{2x^2} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$ $$ = -\frac{1}{2x^2}\ln(x) + \frac{1}{2} \int x^{-3} \, dx $$ $$ = -\frac{1}{2x^2}\ln(x) + \frac{1}{2} \frac{x^{-2}}{-2} + C $$
$$ = -\frac{1}{2x^2}\ln(x) - \frac{1}{4x^2} + C $$

3. Recursive Integration & Alternatives

Ex: $I = \int \frac{\ln|x|}{x} \, dx =$
$u = \ln|x|$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$v = \ln|x|$
$$ = (\ln|x|)^2 - \int \ln|x| \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$

$$ I = (\ln|x|)^2 - I $$ $$ 2I = (\ln|x|)^2 $$ $$ I = \frac{(\ln|x|)^2}{2} $$

Thus,
$$ \int \frac{\ln|x|}{x} \, dx = \frac{(\ln|x|)^2}{2} + C $$

Alternate Method: ($u$-sub)

$$ \int \frac{\ln|x|}{x} \, dx = \int \ln|x| \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$
$u = \ln|x|$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$$ = \int u \cdot du = \frac{u^2}{2} + C $$
$$ = \frac{(\ln|x|)^2}{2} + C $$

4. Inverse Trigonometric Functions

Ex: $\int 5\tan^{-1}(2x) \, dx =$
$u = \tan^{-1}(2x)$
$du = \frac{2}{1+(2x)^2} \, dx$
$dv = 5 \, dx$
$v = 5x$
$$ = 5x\tan^{-1}(2x) - \int \frac{10x}{1+4x^2} \, dx $$
$u = 1+4x^2$
$du = 8x \, dx$
$\frac{1}{8}du = x \, dx$
$$ = 5x\tan^{-1}(2x) - 10 \int \frac{1}{1+4x^2} \cdot x \, dx $$ $$ = 5x\tan^{-1}(2x) - 10 \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{1}{8} \, du $$ $$ = 5x\tan^{-1}(2x) - \frac{10}{8}\ln|u| + C $$
$$ = 5x\tan^{-1}(2x) - \frac{5}{4}\ln|1+4x^2| + C $$

Reminder for homework

$$ (\tan^{-1}(f(x)))' = \frac{f'(x)}{1 + (f(x))^2} $$ $$ (\cot^{-1}(f(x)))' = -\frac{f'(x)}{1 + (f(x))^2} $$ $$ (\sin^{-1}(f(x)))' = \frac{f'(x)}{\sqrt{1 - (f(x))^2}} $$ $$ (\cos^{-1}(f(x)))' = -\frac{f'(x)}{\sqrt{1 - (f(x))^2}} $$ $$ (\sec^{-1}(f(x)))' = \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)\sqrt{(f(x))^2 - 1}} $$ $$ (\csc^{-1}(f(x)))' = -\frac{f'(x)}{f(x)\sqrt{(f(x))^2 - 1}} $$

5. Linear Composition Integrals

Recall

If $\quad \int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + C$,

then $\quad \int f(mx+b) \, dx = \frac{1}{m} F(mx+b) + C$
$$ \int e^{2x} \, dx = \frac{e^{2x}}{2} + C $$ $$ \int e^{x/2} \, dx = \frac{e^{x/2}}{1/2} + C = 2e^{x/2} + C $$ $$ \int \sin(3x+1) \, dx = -\frac{\cos(3x+1)}{3} + C $$ $$ \int \cos(5x) \, dx = \frac{\sin(5x)}{5} + C $$

7. Exponential × Trig Integrals (Looping)

Ex: $I = \int e^x \sin(x) \, dx =$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$dv = e^x \, dx$
$v = e^x$
$$ e^x\sin(x) - \int e^x\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \cos(x)$
$du = -\sin(x) \, dx$
$dv = e^x \, dx$
$v = e^x$
$$ I = e^x\sin(x) - \left( e^x\cos(x) + \int e^x\sin(x) \, dx \right) $$ $$ I = e^x\sin(x) - e^x\cos(x) - I $$ $$ 2I = e^x\sin(x) - e^x\cos(x) $$
$$ I = \frac{e^x}{2}\sin(x) - \frac{e^x}{2}\cos(x) + C $$
Ex: $I = \int e^{2x} \cos(x) \, dx =$
$u = \cos(x)$
$du = -\sin(x) \, dx$
$dv = e^{2x} \, dx$
$v = e^{2x}/2$
$$ \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\cos(x) + \frac{1}{2}\int e^{2x}\sin(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$dv = e^{2x} \, dx$
$v = e^{2x}/2$
$$ I = \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\cos(x) + \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\sin(x) - \frac{1}{2} \int e^{2x}\cos(x) \, dx \right) $$ $$ I = \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\cos(x) + \frac{e^{2x}}{4}\sin(x) - \frac{1}{4}I $$ $$ \frac{5I}{4} = \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\cos(x) + \frac{e^{2x}}{4}\sin(x) $$
$$ I = \frac{4}{5} \left( \frac{e^{2x}}{2}\cos(x) + \frac{e^{2x}}{4}\sin(x) \right) + C $$
