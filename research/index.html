Research

Ramsey theory, ultrafilters, and mathematical foundations.

My research lives near the boundary of combinatorial set theory, topology, logic, and nonstandard methods. I am especially interested in structural Ramsey phenomena and the ways infinitary combinatorics organizes mathematical objects.

Research Areas

  • Topological Ramsey spaces and abstract Ellentuck-style theorems.
  • Ultrafilters, Tukey theory, and Dedekind cuts in nonstandard arithmetic.
  • Ramsey-theoretic and nonstandard approaches to mathematical foundations.
  • Student-facing projects in graph theory, voting theory, and generalized trigonometry.

Documents

  • Doctoral dissertation: Topological Ramsey spaces, associated ultrafilters, and applications.
  • Master's thesis: Attractors for nonlinear reaction-diffusion equations in unbounded domains.
  • Quotient-Ring Trigonometry, with Nathan Collins and John Tierney.
