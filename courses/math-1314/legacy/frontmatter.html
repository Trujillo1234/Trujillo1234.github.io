MATH 1314

Precalculus Algebra

MATH 1314 is a College Algebra course organized around the language of functions and the algebraic habits needed for later work in mathematics, science, business, and quantitative reasoning.

The course builds algebraic fluency around equations, inequalities, functions, models, polynomials, exponentials, and logarithms. The emphasis is on reasoning that can be read by another person: students learn to state what an expression means, justify transformations, interpret graphs, and connect algebraic work to the structure of a problem.

Course Structure

The original PreTeXt course was organized around a progression from mathematical habits into algebraic tools. Students begin with growth mindset and course expectations, then move through equations and inequalities, functions, linear models, polynomial and rational functions, and finally exponential and logarithmic functions. The videos below follow that same path.

  • Introduction to Precalculus: mathematical habits, course expectations, learning outcomes, homework systems, calculator expectations, and support structures.
  • Equations and Inequalities: coordinate graphs, linear equations, applications, complex numbers, quadratic equations, other equation types, and inequalities.
  • Functions: notation, domain and range, rates of change, composition, transformations, absolute value functions, and inverse functions.
  • Linear Functions: linear structure, slope, intercepts, and modeling.
  • Polynomials and Rational Functions: quadratic functions, polynomial graphs, division, zeros, rational functions, and radical functions.
  • Logarithms and Exponentials: exponential functions, logarithmic functions, graph transformations, logarithmic properties, equations, and models.

Video Modules

The videos below are embedded directly on the page and renumbered in the order of the course.

Mathematical Mindset

Video 1

The Truth About Your Brain

An introduction to neuroplasticity and the idea that learning changes the brain. This opening video frames mathematical struggle as productive practice rather than evidence that a student is not a math person.

Video 2

The Power of Yet

Carol Dweck explains the growth mindset and the difference between failure and not yet. The video supports a course culture where students revise, persist, and treat feedback as part of learning.

Video 3

The Growth Mindset

A discussion of fixed and growth mindsets, with attention to process, effort, and resilience. The course uses this language to make mathematical progress visible over time.

Equations and Inequalities

Video 4

2.1 - The Rectangular Coordinate System and Graphs

Introduces ordered pairs, quadrants, coordinate graphs, and x- and y-intercepts. Students practice reading algebraic information from a graph and representing numerical relationships visually.

Functions

Video 5

3.1 - Functions and Function Notation

Explains how to identify functions, interpret function notation, evaluate values such as f(x), and solve for inputs or outputs in context.

Video 6

3.4 - Composition of Functions

Covers arithmetic combinations of functions and composition. The emphasis is on evaluating composite expressions carefully and tracking domain restrictions.

Video 7

3.5 - Transformations of Functions I

Introduces transformations of parent functions, including shifts, reflections, stretches, and compressions. Students connect algebraic changes in formulas to visual changes in graphs.

Video 8

3.5 - Transformations of Functions II

Continues function transformations with additional examples and graphing practice, including symmetry and interpreting transformed parent functions.

Video 9

3.6 - Absolute Value Functions

Focuses on absolute value equations and graphs, including distance interpretations and translations of the absolute value parent function.

Video 10

3.7 - Inverse Functions

Introduces one-to-one functions, the horizontal line test, and algebraic methods for finding and verifying inverse functions.

Polynomial and Rational Functions

Video 11

5.1 - Quadratic Functions

Uses the OpenStax College Algebra text to introduce quadratic functions in standard and general form, with attention to vertices and graph structure.

Video 12

5.1 - Quadratic Functions: Graph Features

Develops the graph of a parabola by identifying the vertex, axis of symmetry, intercepts, and minimum or maximum value.

Video 13

5.2 - Power Functions and Polynomial Functions

Introduces polynomial degree, end behavior, turning points, and the overall shape of polynomial graphs.

Video 14

5.3 - Graphs of Polynomial Functions

Studies zeros, multiplicity, and how polynomial graphs behave near the x-axis. Students learn to connect factored form with graph behavior.

Video 15

5.5 - Zeros of Polynomial Functions

Covers real and complex zeros, complete factorization, and the complex conjugate theorem for polynomial functions.

Video 16

5.6 - Rational Functions

Explains intercepts and asymptotes for rational functions, including vertical, horizontal, and slant asymptotes.

Exponentials and Logarithms

Video 17

6.2 - Graphs of Exponential Functions

Shows how to sketch and transform exponential functions, with attention to domain, range, intercepts, and asymptotic behavior.

Video 18

6.3 - Logarithmic Functions

Introduces logarithms as inverse operations for exponentials and practices converting between exponential and logarithmic form.

Video 19

Graphs of Logarithmic Functions

Develops graphs of logarithmic functions by identifying domain, range, transformations, and vertical asymptotes.

Video 20

6.5 - Logarithmic Properties

Uses product, quotient, and power rules to expand and condense logarithmic expressions.

Video 21

6.6 - Exponential and Logarithmic Equations

Solves equations involving exponential and logarithmic expressions, including equations that require logarithm properties.

Video 22

Exponential Growth and Decay

Applies exponential models to doubling time, half-life, and population growth or decay problems.
