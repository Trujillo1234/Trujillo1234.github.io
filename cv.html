Profile

Associate Professor of Mathematics whose work combines Ramsey theory, ultrafilters, foundations of mathematics, and mathematical logic with teaching innovation in undergraduate and graduate mathematics.

Appointments

  1. 2017-present. Sam Houston State University, Department of Mathematics and Statistics. Associate Professor of Mathematics; promoted in 2025.
  2. 2015-2017. Colorado School of Mines. Postdoctoral Teaching Scholar.
  3. 2014-2015. Texas State University. Lecturer.
  4. 2008-2014. University of Denver. Instructor and graduate teaching assistant.
  5. 2006-2008. New Mexico Tech. Instructor.

Education

  1. 2014. Ph.D. in Mathematics, University of Denver. Dissertation: Topological Ramsey spaces, associated ultrafilters and their applications to the Tukey theory of ultrafilters and Dedekind cuts of nonstandard arithmetic. Advisor: Natasha Dobrinen.
  2. 2008. M.S. in Mathematics, New Mexico Tech. Thesis: Attractors for nonlinear reaction-diffusion equations in unbounded domains. Advisor: Bixiang Wang.
  3. 2006. B.S. in Mathematics, New Mexico Tech, minor in education.

Research Areas

Ramsey theory, topological Ramsey spaces, ultrafilters, nonstandard models of arithmetic, mathematical logic, set theory, mathematical pedagogy, and curriculum design.

Teaching and Course Design

Courses taught include precalculus algebra, college mathematics, trigonometry, calculus I, calculus II, calculus III, differential equations, introduction to mathematical thought, algebraic structures, and mathematical logic.

  • Built workbook-style course systems for differential equations and calculus III, organized around lecture, classwork, homework, quizzes, and review.
  • Maintained public-facing calculus II weekly notes, classwork, homework, and solutions.
  • Redesigned Blackboard course delivery using HTML materials and revised instructional videos.
  • Developed standards-based grading for Algebraic Structures around 42 learning objectives, proof writing, presentations, portfolios, oral exams, quizzes, and exams.
  • Coordinated precalculus algebra and trigonometry with common objectives, shared resources, open educational materials, and structured assessment.

Mentoring

Mentored undergraduate and graduate students through independent studies, capstone work, conference presentations, early research projects, and directed readings in logic, combinatorics, chaos theory, category theory, graph theory, and foundations.
