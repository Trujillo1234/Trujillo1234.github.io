Trigonometric Substitutions

Calculus 2 • Friday Notes
Key Learning Objective

Apply trigonometric substitution to evaluate an antiderivative.

  • Tangent
  • Secant
  • Sine

1. The Core Idea & Algorithm

IDEA:

Anything that has a quadratic expression $$ f(x) = Ax^2 + Bx + C $$ is a potential trigonometric substitution.

Algorithm

Step Action Details / Formulas
1 Complete the square $$ f(x) = A(x-h)^2 + k $$ where $(h,k)$ is the vertex:
$$ h = \frac{-B}{2A}, \quad k = f\left(\frac{-B}{2A}\right) $$
2 Determine the trigonometric substitution
  1. $A$ and $k$ have the same sign.
    $$ x - h = \sqrt{\frac{|k|}{|A|}} \tan(\theta) $$
  2. $A$ is positive and $k$ is negative.
    $$ x - h = \sqrt{\frac{|k|}{|A|}} \sec(\theta) $$
  3. $A$ is negative and $k$ is positive.
    $$ x - h = \sqrt{\frac{|k|}{|A|}} \sin(\theta) $$
3 Apply the trigonometric substitution and simplify Using trigonometric identities:
  1. $ \tan^2(\theta) + 1 = \sec^2(\theta) $
  2. $ \sec^2(\theta) - 1 = \tan^2(\theta) $
  3. $ 1 - \sin^2(\theta) = \cos^2(\theta) $
4 Find antiderivative Find the antiderivative with respect to $\theta$.
5 Convert back to $x$ Draw a right triangle for $\theta$ to convert everything back to $x$.

2. Worked Examples

Example
$$ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+2x+5}} \, dx $$
Solution (Completing the square):

$h = \frac{-B}{2A} = \frac{-2}{2(1)} = -1$
$k = f(-1) = (-1)^2 + 2(-1) + 5 = 4$

So, $x^2+2x+5 = (x+1)^2 + 4$
Trigonometric Substitution:

$x + 1 = \sqrt{\frac{4}{1}} \tan(\theta)$
$x + 1 = 2\tan(\theta)$
$dx = 2\sec^2(\theta) \, d\theta$
$$ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+2x+5}} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{(x+1)^2+4}} \, dx $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\tan^2(\theta)+4}} \, 2\sec^2(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\sec^2(\theta)}} \, 2\sec^2(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{2\sec(\theta)} \, 2\sec^2(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \sec(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \ln|\sec(\theta) + \tan(\theta)| + C $$
Draw Triangle
$x + 1 = 2\tan(\theta)$
$\frac{x+1}{2} = \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$
$\theta$
$2$
$x+1$
$\sqrt{(x+1)^2+4}$
$\sec(\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{(x+1)^2+4}}{2}$
$$ = \ln\left| \frac{\sqrt{(x+1)^2+4}}{2} + \frac{x+1}{2} \right| + C $$
Example
$$ \int \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^2-1}} \, dx $$
Note:

$x^2 - 1 = (x-0)^2 - 1$

i.e. $h=0, \; k=-1, \; A=1$
Trigonometric Substitution:

$x = \sqrt{\frac{|-1|}{1}} \sec(\theta)$
$x = \sec(\theta)$
$dx = \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) \, d\theta$
$$ \int \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^2-1}} \, dx = \int \frac{\sec^2(\theta)}{\sqrt{\sec^2(\theta)-1}} \, \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{\sec^2(\theta)}{\tan(\theta)} \, \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \sec^3(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \frac{1}{2} \left( \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) + \ln|\sec(\theta) + \tan(\theta)| \right) + C $$
Draw Triangle
$\frac{x}{1} = \sec(\theta) = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{adjacent}}$
$\theta$
$1$
$\sqrt{x^2-1}$
$x$
$\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \sqrt{x^2 - 1}$
$$ = \frac{1}{2} \left( x\sqrt{x^2-1} + \ln\left| x + \sqrt{x^2-1} \right| \right) + C $$
Example
$$ \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{18-2x^2}} \, dx $$
Completing the square:

$18 - 2x^2 = -2(x-0)^2 + 18$

i.e. $h=0, \; A=-2, \; k=18$
Trigonometric Substitution:

$x = \sqrt{\frac{|18|}{|-2|}} \sin(\theta)$
$x = \sqrt{9} \sin(\theta)$
$x = 3\sin(\theta)$
$dx = 3\cos(\theta) \, d\theta$
$$ \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{18-2x^2}} \, dx = \int \frac{3\sin(\theta)}{\sqrt{18 - 18\sin^2(\theta)}} \, 3\cos(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{3\sin(\theta)}{\sqrt{18 \cos^2(\theta)}} \, 3\cos(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{9\sin(\theta)}{\sqrt{18} \cos(\theta)} \, \cos(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = \int \frac{9}{\sqrt{18}} \sin(\theta) \, d\theta $$ $$ = -\frac{9}{\sqrt{18}}\cos(\theta) + C $$
Draw a Picture
$\sin(\theta) = \frac{x}{3} = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$
$\theta$
$\sqrt{9-x^2}$
$x$
$3$
$\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{\sqrt{9-x^2}}{3}$
$$ = -\frac{9}{\sqrt{18}} \left( \frac{\sqrt{9-x^2}}{3} \right) + C $$
$$ = -\frac{3}{\sqrt{18}}\sqrt{9-x^2} + C $$
