Problem 1
$$ \int \frac{x^3 - x^2 + 2x + 3}{x + 1} \, dx $$

Since the degree of the numerator (3) is greater than or equal to the degree of the denominator (1), we begin by performing polynomial long division.

$$ \begin{array}{r} x^2 - 2x + 4 \\ x + 1 \overline{ ) \ x^3 - x^2 + 2x + 3} \\ \underline{-(x^3 + x^2)} \phantom{{} + 2x + 3\ } \\ -2x^2 + 2x + 3 \\ \underline{-(-2x^2 - 2x)} \phantom{{} + 3\ } \\ 4x + 3 \\ \underline{-(4x + 4)} \\ -1 \end{array} $$
$$ \int \frac{x^3 - x^2 + 2x + 3}{x + 1} \, dx = \int \left( x^2 - 2x + 4 - \frac{1}{x + 1} \right) dx $$
$$ = \frac{x^3}{3} - x^2 + 4x - \ln|x + 1| + C $$
Problem 2
$$ \int \frac{7x + 1}{(x + 1)(x - 2)} \, dx $$
Partial Fractions:

$$ \frac{7x + 1}{(x + 1)(x - 2)} = \frac{A}{x + 1} + \frac{B}{x - 2} $$ $$ 7x + 1 = A(x - 2) + B(x + 1) $$

Let $x = 2$:

$$ 7(2) + 1 = A\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(2 - 2)} + B(2 + 1) $$ $$ 15 = 3B $$ $$ \frac{15}{3} = B \implies 5 = B $$

Let $x = -1$:

$$ 7(-1) + 1 = A(-1 - 2) + B\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(-1 + 1)} $$ $$ -6 = -3A $$ $$ 2 = A $$

Thus,

$$ \int \frac{7x + 1}{(x + 1)(x - 2)} \, dx = \int \left( \frac{2}{x + 1} + \frac{5}{x - 2} \right) dx $$
$$ = 2\ln|x + 1| + 5\ln|x - 2| + C $$

Problem 4
$$ \int \frac{x + 7}{(x - 1)(x + 3)} \, dx $$
Partial Fractions:

$$ \frac{x + 7}{(x - 1)(x + 3)} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{B}{x + 3} $$ $$ x + 7 = A(x + 3) + B(x - 1) $$

Let $x = -3$:

$$ -3 + 7 = A\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(-3 + 3)} + B(-3 - 1) $$ $$ 4 = -4B $$ $$ -1 = B $$

Let $x = 1$:

$$ 1 + 7 = A(1 + 3) + B\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(1 - 1)} $$ $$ 8 = 4A $$ $$ 2 = A $$

Thus,

$$ \int \frac{x + 7}{(x - 1)(x + 3)} \, dx = \int \left( \frac{2}{x - 1} - \frac{1}{x + 3} \right) dx $$
$$ = 2\ln|x - 1| - \ln|x + 3| + C $$
Problem 5
$$ \int \left( \frac{3}{(x + 2)^2} + \frac{4}{x + 2} \right) dx $$

Work term-by-term:

First term:

$$ \int \frac{3}{(x + 2)^2} \, dx $$
$u = x + 2$
$du = dx$
$$ = \int \frac{3}{u^2} \, du = \int 3u^{-2} \, du = \frac{3u^{-1}}{-1} + C $$ $$ = -\frac{3}{u} + C = -\frac{3}{x + 2} + C $$

Second term:

$$ \int \frac{4}{x + 2} \, dx = 4\ln|x + 2| + C $$

Thus,

$$ \int \left( \frac{3}{(x + 2)^2} + \frac{4}{x + 2} \right) dx $$
$$ = -\frac{3}{x + 2} + 4\ln|x + 2| + C $$

Problem 10

Partial Fractions:

$$ \frac{2x - 4}{x(x - 4)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x - 4} $$ $$ 2x - 4 = A(x - 4) + Bx $$

Let $x = 4$:

$$ 2(4) - 4 = A\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(4 - 4)} + B \cdot 4 $$ $$ 4 = 4B $$ $$ 1 = B $$

Let $x = 0$:

$$ 2(0) - 4 = A(0 - 4) + B\cancelto{\text{zero}}{ \cdot 0} $$ $$ -4 = -4A $$ $$ 1 = A $$

$$ \int \frac{2x - 4}{x(x - 4)} \, dx = \int \left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{x - 4} \right) dx $$

Problem 13
$$ \int \ln(x^2 - 1) \, dx $$
Integration by parts!

$u = \ln(x^2 - 1) \implies du = \frac{2x}{x^2 - 1} \, dx$
$dv = dx \implies v = x$
$$ \int \ln(x^2 - 1) \, dx = x\ln(x^2 - 1) - \int \frac{2x^2}{x^2 - 1} \, dx $$
Long Division:

$$ \begin{array}{r} 2 \phantom{x^2} \\ x^2 + 0x - 1 \overline{ ) \ 2x^2 + 0x + 0} \\ \underline{-(2x^2 + 0x - 2)} \\ 2 \end{array} $$
Partial Fractions:

$$ \frac{2}{(x + 1)(x - 1)} = \frac{A}{x + 1} + \frac{B}{x - 1} $$ $$ 2 = A(x - 1) + B(x + 1) $$

Let $x = 1$:

$2 = A\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(1 - 1)} + B(1 + 1) \implies 2 = 2B \implies 1 = B$

Let $x = -1$:

$2 = A(-1 - 1) + B\cancelto{\text{zero}}{(-1 + 1)} \implies 2 = -2A \implies -1 = A$
$$ = x\ln(x^2 - 1) - \int \left( 2 + \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} \right) dx $$ $$ = x\ln(x^2 - 1) - 2x - \int \frac{2}{(x + 1)(x - 1)} \, dx $$

Thus,

$$ \int \ln(x^2 - 1) \, dx = x\ln(x^2 - 1) - 2x - \int \left( \frac{-1}{x + 1} + \frac{1}{x - 1} \right) dx $$
$$ = x\ln(x^2 - 1) - 2x + \ln|x + 1| - \ln|x - 1| + C $$
