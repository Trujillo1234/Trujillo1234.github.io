Homework 9 Solutions

Calculus 2 • Work & Centroids
  1. A $20$ meter cable hangs from the ceiling. The cable has a density of $2 \text{ N/m}$. How much work is required to lift the entire cable to the ceiling i.e. so that none is hanging?
    Solution
    Let $x$ be the amount of rope left hanging. Then the variable force is $$ F(x)=2x. $$ So the total work is $$ W=\int_0^{20}2x\,dx =x^2\Big|_0^{20} =(20)^2-0^2 =400. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{400\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}}$
  2. A chain weighs $5$ pounds per foot and is initially lying coiled on the ground. How much work is needed to lift the chain so that it hangs vertically with its bottom touching the ground, fully extended to a height of $25 \text{ ft}$?
    Solution
    Let $x$ be the amount of chain hanging in air. Then the variable force is $$ F(x)=5x. $$ Hence $$ W=\int_0^{25}5x\,dx =\frac{5x^2}{2}\Big|_0^{25} =\frac{5(25)^2}{2}-\frac{5(0)^2}{2} =1562.5. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{1562.5\text{ ft}\cdot\text{lb}}$
  3. How much work is needed to compress a spring ($k = 25 \text{ kg/s}^2$) $5.5 \text{ cm}$ from equilibrium?
    Solution
    First convert to meters: $$ 5.5\text{ cm}=0.055\text{ m}. $$ Since the spring is compressed, use the negative displacement $-0.055$. By Hooke's Law, $$ F(x)=25x. $$ Then $$ W=\int_0^{-0.055}25x\,dx =\frac{25x^2}{2}\Big|_0^{-0.055} =\frac{25(-0.055)^2}{2}-\frac{25(0)^2}{2} \approx 0.0378. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{0.0378\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}}$
  4. A rectangular trough is $2$ feet wide, $15$ feet long, and $2$ feet deep. It is filled with water, which weighs $62.4$ pound per cubic foot. How much work is required to pump all the water to the top of the trough?
    Solution
    Use horizontal slices of thickness $\Delta y$. The volume of the $i$th slab is $$ V=2\cdot 15\cdot \Delta y=30\Delta y. $$ Its weight is $$ 62.4(30\Delta y)=1872\Delta y. $$ A slice at height $y_i$ must be lifted $$ 2-y_i $$ feet. So the total work is $$ W=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n 1872(2-y_i)\Delta y =\int_0^2 1872(2-y)\,dy. $$ Now compute: $$ W=1872\left(2y-\frac{y^2}{2}\right)\Big|_0^2 =1872\left(2(2)-\frac{(2)^2}{2}\right)-0 =1872(4-2) =3744. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{3744\text{ ft}\cdot\text{lb}}$
  5. A tank is formed by rotating $y = \sqrt{400-x^2}$ on $[0,20]$ about the $y$-axis. The base is also fully enclosed, and the $x$ and $y$ axes are measured in meters. The tank is filled with diesel fuel of density $8240 \text{ N/m}^3$. How much work is needed to pump the liquid $10 \text{ meters}$ above the tank?
    Solution
    Use horizontal slices of thickness $\Delta y$. At height $y_i$, the radius is $$ r=\sqrt{400-y_i^2}. $$ So the volume of the $i$th slab is $$ V=\pi r^2\Delta y =\pi(400-y_i^2)\Delta y. $$ Its weight is $$ 8240\pi(400-y_i^2)\Delta y. $$ Since the liquid is pumped $10$ meters above the top of the tank, a slice at height $y_i$ must be lifted $$ 30-y_i $$ meters. Therefore $$ W=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n 8240\pi(400-y_i^2)(30-y_i)\Delta y =\int_0^{20}8240\pi(400-y^2)(30-y)\,dy. $$ Expanding: $$ W=8240\pi\int_0^{20}(12000-400y-30y^2+y^3)\,dy. $$ Now compute: $$ W=8240\pi\left(12000y-200y^2-10y^3+\frac{y^4}{4}\right)\Big|_0^{20} $$ $$ =8240\pi\left(12000(20)-200(20)^2-10(20)^3+\frac{(20)^4}{4}\right) $$ $$ =8240\pi(240000-80000-80000+40000) $$ $$ =8240\pi(120000) =988{,}800{,}000\pi. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{988{,}800{,}000\pi\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}}$
  6. How much work is needed to stretch a spring with $k = 5 \text{ lbs/ft}$ $15 \text{ cm}$ from its equilibrium position?
    Solution
    Convert first: $$ 15\text{ cm}\approx 0.49\text{ ft}. $$ By Hooke's Law, $$ F(x)=5x. $$ So $$ W=\int_0^{0.49}5x\,dx =\frac{5x^2}{2}\Big|_0^{0.49} =\frac{5(0.49)^2}{2}-0 \approx 0.605. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{0.605\text{ ft}\cdot\text{lb}}$
  7. A rope weighing $1/4 \text{ lb/ft}$ and totaling $16 \text{ feet}$ lies coiled on the ground. The midpoint ($8 \text{ feet}$ from each end) is lifted vertically until the two ends touch the ground, forming two fully extended $8 \text{ ft}$ segments. How much work was needed to lift the rope to this position?
    Solution
    Let $x$ be the distance the midpoint is above the ground. Then there are two halves of rope in air of length $x$ each, so the total rope in air is $$ x+x=2x. $$ Since the rope weighs $\frac14\text{ lb/ft}$, the variable force is $$ F(x)=\frac14(2x)=\frac{x}{2}. $$ Hence $$ W=\int_0^8 \frac{x}{2}\,dx =\frac{x^2}{4}\Big|_0^8 =\frac{(8)^2}{4}-0 =16. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{16\text{ ft}\cdot\text{lb}}$
  8. A $30 \text{ ft}$ chain hangs vertically from the ceiling and weighs $5$ pounds per foot. How much work is required to lift the bottom end of the chain up the ceiling, so that the chain is folded in half and both ends are hanging side-by-side from the ceiling, fully suspended?
    Solution
    Use horizontal pieces of thickness $\Delta y$ measured from the ground upward. Let $$ \Delta y=\frac{15}{n}. $$ A small piece of chain has weight $$ 5\Delta y. $$ A piece originally at height $y_i$ moves to height $30-y_i$, so the distance it is lifted is $$ (30-y_i)-y_i=30-2y_i. $$ Therefore the work is $$ W=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n 5(30-2y_i)\Delta y =\int_0^{15}5(30-2y)\,dy. $$ Now compute: $$ W=5(30y-y^2)\Big|_0^{15} =5\left(30(15)-(15)^2\right)-0 =5(450-225) =1125. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{1125\text{ ft}\cdot\text{lb}}$
  9. A tank is formed by rotating $x = \sqrt{100-(y-10)^2}$ on $[0,10]$ about the $y$-axis. The $x$ and $y$ axes are measured in meters. The tank is filled with fuel of density $8240 \text{ N/m}^3$. How much work is needed to pump the liquid to the top of the tank?
    Solution
    Use horizontal slices of thickness $\Delta y$. Since $$ r=\sqrt{100-(y_i-10)^2}, $$ we have $$ r^2=100-(y_i-10)^2. $$ Expanding: $$ r^2=100-(y_i^2-20y_i+100)=-y_i^2+20y_i. $$ So the volume of the $i$th slab is $$ V=\pi r^2\Delta y =\pi(-y_i^2+20y_i)\Delta y. $$ Its weight is $$ 8240\pi(-y_i^2+20y_i)\Delta y. $$ A slice at height $y_i$ must be lifted to the top of the tank, so the distance is $$ 20-y_i. $$ Hence $$ W=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n 8240\pi(-y_i^2+20y_i)(20-y_i)\Delta y =\int_0^{20}8240\pi(-y^2+20y)(20-y)\,dy. $$ Expanding: $$ W=8240\pi\int_0^{20}(y^3-40y^2+400y)\,dy. $$ Now compute: $$ W=8240\pi\left(\frac{y^4}{4}-\frac{40y^3}{3}+200y^2\right)\Big|_0^{20} $$ $$ =8240\pi\left(\frac{(20)^4}{4}-\frac{40(20)^3}{3}+200(20)^2\right) $$ $$ =8240\pi\left(40000-\frac{320000}{3}+80000\right) $$ $$ =8240\pi\left(\frac{120000-320000+240000}{3}\right) =8240\pi\left(\frac{40000}{3}\right) =\frac{329{,}600{,}000\pi}{3}. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{\dfrac{329{,}600{,}000\pi}{3}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}}$
  10. A spring with $k = 500 \text{ kg/s}^2$ is initially compressed $2 \text{ cm}$ from equilibrium. How much work is needed to stretch the spring $5 \text{ cm}$ past equilibrium?
    Solution
    Convert the endpoints to meters: $$ -2\text{ cm}=-0.02\text{ m}, \qquad 5\text{ cm}=0.05\text{ m}. $$ By Hooke's Law, $$ F(x)=500x. $$ Then $$ W=\int_{-0.02}^{0.05}500x\,dx =250x^2\Big|_{-0.02}^{0.05} $$ $$ =250(0.05)^2-250(-0.02)^2 =250(0.0025)-250(0.0004) =0.625-0.1 =0.525. $$
    Answer: $\boxed{0.525\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}}$
  11. Find the centroid of each region.
    1. Solution
      The region is bounded by $$ y=\sqrt{x} \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=x. $$ Area: $$ A=\int_0^1(\sqrt{x}-x)\,dx =\left(\frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}-\frac{x^2}{2}\right)\Big|_0^1 =\frac{2}{3}-\frac{1}{2} =\frac{1}{6}. $$ Numerator of $\bar{x}$: $$ \int_0^1 x(\sqrt{x}-x)\,dx =\int_0^1(x^{3/2}-x^2)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(\frac{2}{5}x^{5/2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right)\Big|_0^1 =\frac{2}{5}-\frac{1}{3} =\frac{1}{15}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{x}=\frac{\frac{1}{15}}{\frac{1}{6}}=\frac{2}{5}. $$ Numerator of $\bar{y}$: $$ \int_0^1 \frac{(\sqrt{x})^2-x^2}{2}\,dx =\int_0^1\left(\frac{x}{2}-\frac{x^2}{2}\right)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(\frac{x^2}{4}-\frac{x^3}{6}\right)\Big|_0^1 =\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{6} =\frac{1}{12}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{y}=\frac{\frac{1}{12}}{\frac{1}{6}}=\frac{1}{2}. $$
      Answer: $\boxed{\left(\frac{2}{5},\frac{1}{2}\right)}$
    2. Solution
      The region is bounded by $$ y=\frac{1}{x} \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=2-x $$ on $[1,2]$. Area: $$ A=\int_1^2\left(\frac{1}{x}-(2-x)\right)\,dx =\int_1^2\left(\frac{1}{x}-2+x\right)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(\ln|x|-2x+\frac{x^2}{2}\right)\Big|_1^2 =(\ln 2-4+2)-(\ln 1-2+\tfrac12) =\ln 2-\frac12. $$ Numerator of $\bar{x}$: $$ \int_1^2 x\left(\frac{1}{x}-(2-x)\right)\,dx =\int_1^2(1-2x+x^2)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(x-x^2+\frac{x^3}{3}\right)\Big|_1^2 =\frac{1}{3}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{x}=\frac{\frac{1}{3}}{\ln 2-\frac12}\approx 1.726. $$ Numerator of $\bar{y}$: $$ \int_1^2 \frac{\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^2-(2-x)^2}{2}\,dx =\int_1^2\left(\frac{1}{2x^2}-\frac{4-4x+x^2}{2}\right)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(-\frac{1}{2x}-2x+x^2-\frac{x^3}{6}\right)\Big|_1^2 =\frac{1}{12}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{y}=\frac{\frac{1}{12}}{\ln 2-\frac12}\approx 0.431. $$
      Answer: $\boxed{(1.726,\ 0.431)}$
    3. Solution
      The region is under $$ y=9-x^2 $$ and above the $x$-axis on $[0,3]$. Area: $$ A=\int_0^3(9-x^2)\,dx =\left(9x-\frac{x^3}{3}\right)\Big|_0^3 =27-9 =18. $$ Numerator of $\bar{x}$: $$ \int_0^3 x(9-x^2)\,dx =\int_0^3(9x-x^3)\,dx $$ $$ =\left(\frac{9x^2}{2}-\frac{x^4}{4}\right)\Big|_0^3 =\frac{81}{4}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{x}=\frac{\frac{81}{4}}{18}=\frac{9}{8}. $$ Numerator of $\bar{y}$: $$ \int_0^3 \frac{(9-x^2)^2}{2}\,dx =\int_0^3 \frac{81-18x^2+x^4}{2}\,dx $$ $$ =\left(\frac{81x}{2}-3x^3+\frac{x^5}{10}\right)\Big|_0^3 =\frac{324}{5}. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{y}=\frac{\frac{324}{5}}{18}=\frac{18}{5}. $$
      Answer: $\boxed{\left(\frac{9}{8},\frac{18}{5}\right)}$
    4. Solution
      The region is bounded by $$ y=x \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=-\frac{x}{2} $$ on $[0,2]$. Area: $$ A=\int_0^2\left(x-\left(-\frac{x}{2}\right)\right)\,dx =\int_0^2\frac{3x}{2}\,dx =\frac{3x^2}{4}\Big|_0^2 =3. $$ Numerator of $\bar{x}$: $$ \int_0^2 x\left(x-\left(-\frac{x}{2}\right)\right)\,dx =\int_0^2\frac{3}{2}x^2\,dx $$ $$ =\frac{x^3}{2}\Big|_0^2 =4. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{x}=\frac{4}{3}. $$ Numerator of $\bar{y}$: $$ \int_0^2 \frac{x^2-\left(-\frac{x}{2}\right)^2}{2}\,dx =\int_0^2\left(\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^2}{8}\right)\,dx $$ $$ =\int_0^2\frac{3x^2}{8}\,dx =\frac{x^3}{8}\Big|_0^2 =1. $$ Therefore $$ \bar{y}=\frac{1}{3}. $$
      Answer: $\boxed{\left(\frac{4}{3},\frac{1}{3}\right)}$
