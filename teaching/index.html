Teaching

Organized, intellectually serious, and supportive of real student growth.

My teaching is grounded in active learning, backward design, and careful alignment among learning objectives, class activities, and assessment. I want students to calculate, but also to explain, compare, justify, prove, model, and communicate mathematics.

Course Design

I build courses as coherent learning systems: objectives, guided practice, homework, quizzes, exams, and feedback all point toward the same mathematical habits.

Active Learning

Class time is structured around problem solving, board work, discussion, and frequent checks for understanding rather than passive note-taking alone.

Mentorship

I have mentored undergraduate and graduate students through independent studies, capstones, conference presentations, and early research projects.
