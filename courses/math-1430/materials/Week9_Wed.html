1. Physics Definition of Work

Definition

Work is the transfer of Energy by a force acting.

When force is constant:

$$ \text{work} = (\text{force}) \cdot (\text{distance}) $$
units of work:
Newton - meters
$(N \cdot m)$
↪ Joules (J)
foot - pound
(ft-lbs)

2. Work Under a Variable Force

What happens when the force changes? We represent the force at a distance $x$ as a function $F(x)$.

We cut up the x-axis into $n$-parts to approximate the work:

$$ \Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n} $$

Let $w_i = \text{work required to move from } x_{i-1} \text{ to } x_i$.

$$ \text{distance} = \Delta x $$ $$ \text{Force} \approx F(x_i) $$ $$ w_i \approx F(x_i)\Delta x $$ $$ \text{total work} \approx \sum_{i=1}^n F(x_i)\Delta x $$

Thus,

$$ \text{Total work} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n F(x_i)\Delta x = \int_a^b F(x) \,dx $$

↪ work under a variable force.

3. Hooke's Law (Springs)

For a spring stretched or compressed from equilibrium, the force is given by Hooke's Law.

$$ \text{total work moving from } x=a \text{ to } x=b \quad = \quad \int_a^b kx \,dx $$
Example:

Assume $k = 400 \text{ kg/s}^2$. Find the work required to compress the spring an additional 2 cm when it is already compressed 3 cm.

$$ \text{total work} = \int_{-0.03}^{-0.05} 400x \,dx = \left. 200x^2 \right|_{-0.03}^{-0.05} $$ $$ = 200(-0.05)^2 - 200(-0.03)^2 $$
$$ = 0.32 \text{ kg} \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}^2} \cdot \text{m} = \text{N} \cdot \text{m} $$

4. Lifting a Chain

Example:

How much work is done lifting a 30 foot chain over the side of a building if the chain has a density of $1/2 \text{ lb per foot}$?

Method 1: Variable Force

$x = \text{length of rope left hanging off building in feet.}$

$F(x) = \text{weight of chain} = \frac{1}{2}x \text{ lbs}$

$F(x)$ is a variable force. So,

$$ \text{total work} = \int_0^{30} \frac{1}{2}x \,dx = \left. \frac{1}{2}\frac{x^2}{2} \right|_0^{30} $$ $$ = \frac{30^2}{4} - \frac{0^2}{4} = \frac{900}{4} $$
$$ = 225 \text{ ft-lbs} $$

Method 2: Riemann Sum

$w_i = \text{work from moving } i^{\text{th}} \text{ part of chain to the top of building}$

$= (\text{Force}) \cdot (\text{distance})$

$= \left(\frac{1}{2}\Delta x\right) \cdot (x_i) \quad = \quad \frac{1}{2} x_i \cdot \Delta x$

$$ \text{Total work} \approx w_1 + w_2 + w_3 + w_4 + w_5 = \sum_{i=1}^5 w_i $$

$$ \text{Total work} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{1}{2} x_i \Delta x $$ $$ = \int_0^{30} \frac{1}{2}x \,dx = \left. \frac{x^2}{4} \right|_0^{30} = \frac{30^2}{4} - \frac{0^2}{4} $$
$$ = 225 \text{ ft-lbs} $$
