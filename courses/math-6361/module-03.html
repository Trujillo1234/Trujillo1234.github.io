Module 3

Logic of Boolean Connectives

Truth tables, logical equivalence, tautologies, contradictions, and validity for Boolean forms.

Guiding Questions

  • What are tautologies? What are logical truths?
  • How are tautologies and logical truths related to each other?
  • How do we test for tautology?
  • Is it possible for tautologically equivalent sentences not to be logically equivalent?
  • Is it possible for sentences that are not tautologically equivalent to be logically equivalent?
  • How are tautological equivalence and logical equivalence related?
  • How do we check for tautological equivalence between two first-order sentences?
  • What is a literal?
  • Does every formula have an equivalent formula built from literals using only âˆ§ and âˆ¨?
  • What is conjunctive normal form (CNF), and what is disjunctive normal form (DNF)?

Objectives

  • Compare and contrast the concepts of logical consequence and tautological consequence.
  • Compare and contrast the concepts of logical equivalence and tautological equivalence.
  • Compare and contrast logical truths and tautologies.
  • Recognize tautologies using truth tables.
