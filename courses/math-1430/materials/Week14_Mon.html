Rational Functions

Calculus 2 • Monday Notes (4-20)
Key Learning Objective

Find the antiderivative of a rational function: $$ \int \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} \, dx \quad \leftarrow \frac{\text{polynomial}}{\text{polynomial}} $$

1. General Strategy

Algorithm

Step 1 If $\deg(P(x)) \ge \deg(Q(x))$, then perform long division.

We want to divide the numerator by the denominator to rewrite the expression as a polynomial plus a proper rational function where the remainder's degree is less than the denominator's degree. $$ \int \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} \, dx = \int D(x) + \frac{R(x)}{Q(x)} \, dx $$ Where:
  • $D(x)$ is a polynomial.
  • $\deg(R(x)) < \deg(Q(x))$.

Long Division Setup: $$ \begin{array}{r} D(x) \\ Q(x) \overline{ ) \ P(x)} \\ \vdots \quad \\ \hline R(x) \end{array} $$
Step 2 Consider the degree of $Q(x)$.

I. $Q(x)$ is degree one.
Use a standard $u$-substitution: $u = Q(x)$.

II. $Q(x)$ is degree two.
$Q(x) = Ax^2 + Bx + C$

  1. $Q(x)$ factors into distinct factors $\hookrightarrow$ (Partial Fractions)
  2. $Q(x)$ has a repeated factor $\hookrightarrow$ ($u$-sub & algebra)
  3. $Q(x)$ does not factor (irreducible) $\hookrightarrow$ (complete the square & trig. sub)
    $\hookrightarrow$ (Always tangent sub)

Does it factor? Check the Discriminant

For $Q(x) = Ax^2 + Bx + C$:

  • (a) $B^2 - 4AC$ is positive $\longrightarrow$ two distinct factors (partial fractions)
  • (b) $B^2 - 4AC$ is zero $\longrightarrow$ repeated factor ($u$-sub & algebra)
  • (c) $B^2 - 4AC$ is negative $\longrightarrow$ irreducible (complete the square and $u$-sub / trig sub)

2. Worked Examples

Example
$$ \int \frac{x^3 - 4x^2 - x + 7}{x^2 - 7x + 12} \, dx $$

Since the degree of the numerator (3) is greater than or equal to the degree of the denominator (2), we begin by performing polynomial long division.

$$ \begin{array}{r} x + 3 \phantom{x^2} \\ x^2 - 7x + 12 \overline{ ) \ x^3 - 4x^2 - x + 7} \\ \underline{-(x^3 - 7x^2 + 12x)} \phantom{{} + 7\ } \\ 3x^2 - 13x + 7 \\ \underline{-(3x^2 - 21x + 36)} \\ 8x - 29 \end{array} $$

Rewriting the integral using the quotient and remainder:

$$ = \int \left( x + 3 + \frac{8x - 29}{x^2 - 7x + 12} \right) dx $$

Next, we try to factor the denominator for the fractional part:

$$ x^2 - 7x + 12 = (x - 3)(x - 4) $$
Partial Fractions Setup:

We set up our partial fractions equation with unknown constants $A$ and $B$: $$ \frac{8x - 29}{(x-3)(x-4)} = \frac{A}{x-3} + \frac{B}{x-4} $$ Multiplying through by the common denominator yields: $$ 8x - 29 = A(x-4) + B(x-3) $$

Let $x = 4$:

$$ \hookrightarrow 8(4) - 29 = A\cancelto{0}{(4-4)} + B(4-3) $$ $$ 3 = B $$

Let $x = 3$:

$$ \hookrightarrow 8(3) - 29 = A(3-4) + B\cancelto{0}{(3-3)} $$ $$ -5 = -A $$ $$ 5 = A $$

Substitute the constants back into the integral:

$$ = \int \left( x + 3 + \frac{5}{x-3} + \frac{3}{x-4} \right) dx $$
$$ = \frac{x^2}{2} + 3x + 5\ln|x - 3| + 3\ln|x - 4| + C $$
Example
$$ \int \frac{5x + 1}{x^2 + 2x + 1} \, dx $$

No need for long division since $\deg(5x+1) = 1 < \deg(x^2+2x+1) = 2$.

Try to factor denominator:

$$ x^2 + 2x + 1 = (x+1)(x+1) = (x+1)^2 \quad \leftarrow \text{repeated root} $$

Rewriting the integral:

$$ \int \frac{5x + 1}{(x+1)^2} \, dx $$
u-substitution:

$u = x + 1 \implies u - 1 = x$
$du = dx$

Substitute $u$ and $x$ into the integral to solve algebraically:

$$ = \int \frac{5(u-1) + 1}{u^2} \, du $$ $$ = \int \frac{5u - 5 + 1}{u^2} \, du $$ $$ = \int \frac{5u - 4}{u^2} \, du $$

Split the fraction:

$$ = \int \left( \frac{5}{u} - 4u^{-2} \right) du $$ $$ = 5\ln|u| + 4u^{-1} + C $$

Substitute back $u = x+1$:

$$ = 5\ln|x+1| + \frac{4}{x+1} + C $$
