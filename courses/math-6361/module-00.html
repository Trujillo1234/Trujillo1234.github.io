Module 0

Atomic Sentences

Names, predicates, functions, atomic formulas, and the first pieces of formal language.

Guiding Questions

  • What are individual constants, and what do these constants refer to?
  • What are predicate symbols used to express?
  • What is an atomic sentence?
  • What are the unary predicate symbols in Tarski's World? What are the binary and ternary predicates that describe the objects' sizes, shapes, and locations?
  • Is it possible for a FOL sentence to be true some of the time and false other times? Why or why not?
  • What do function symbols do? In what ways are function symbols different from predicate symbols, and in what ways are they similar?
  • What factors should we consider when we design a language for discussing a particular subject?
  • What are the similarities and differences between the first-order language of set theory and the first-order language of arithmetic?

Objectives

  • Identify when an expression is an atomic sentence in a general first-order language
  • Identify when an expression is a term (complex term) in a general first-order language
  • Translate between colloquial English and atomic sentences of a general first-order language
  • Compare and contrast the following symbols from first-order logic: constant, predicate, and function
  • Construct terms and atomic sentences in the first-order language of set theory
  • Construct terms and atomic sentences in the first-order language of arithmetic
