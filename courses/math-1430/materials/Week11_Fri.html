Trigonometric Integrals

Calculus 2 • Friday Notes
Key Learning Objective

Evaluate integrals of the form: $$ \int \sin^n(x) \cos^m(x) \, dx $$ where $n$ and $m$ are non-negative integers.

1. Foundations

Recall

$$ \int \cos(x) \, dx = \sin(x) + C $$ $$ \int \sin(x) \, dx = -\cos(x) + C $$ $$ (\sin(x))' = \cos(x) $$ $$ (\cos(x))' = -\sin(x) $$

2. Introductory U-Substitution Examples

Example ($n=1, m=1$)
$$ \int \sin(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$$ = \int u \, du $$ $$ = \frac{u^2}{2} + C $$
$$ = \frac{\sin^2(x)}{2} + C $$
Example ($n=2, m=1$)
$$ \int \sin^2(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$$ = \int u^2 \, du $$ $$ = \frac{u^3}{3} + C $$
$$ = \frac{\sin^3(x)}{3} + C $$
Example ($n=99, m=1$)
$$ \int 100\sin^{99}(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$$ = \int 100u^{99} \, du $$ $$ = \frac{100u^{100}}{100} + C $$ $$ = u^{100} + C $$
$$ = \sin^{100}(x) + C $$

3. Separating Powers

Example ($n=2, m=3$)
$$ \int \sin^2(x)\cos^3(x) \, dx $$ $$ = \int \sin^2(x)\cos^2(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$ $$ = \int \sin^2(x)(1 - \sin^2(x))\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$$ = \int u^2(1 - u^2) \, du $$ $$ = \int (u^2 - u^4) \, du $$ $$ = \frac{u^3}{3} - \frac{u^5}{5} + C $$
$$ = \frac{\sin^3(x)}{3} - \frac{\sin^5(x)}{5} + C $$

Case 1 ($m$ is odd)

  1. Move one $\cos(x)$ next to $dx$: $$ \int \sin^n(x)\cos^{m-1}(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$
  2. Rewrite $\cos^{m-1}(x)$ in terms of $\sin(x)$ using: $$ \cos^2(x) = 1 - \sin^2(x) $$
  3. Apply the $u$-sub   $\mathbf{u = \sin(x)}$   to find the antiderivative.
Example ($n=3, m=5$)
$$ \int \sin^3(x)\cos^5(x) \, dx = \int \sin^3(x)\cos^4(x)\cos(x) \, dx $$ $$ = \int \sin^3(x)(1 - \sin^2(x))(1 - \sin^2(x))\cos(x) \, dx $$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$$ = \int u^3(1 - u^2)(1 - u^2) \, du $$ $$ = \int u^3(1 - 2u^2 + u^4) \, du $$ $$ = \int (u^3 - 2u^5 + u^7) \, du $$ $$ = \frac{u^4}{4} - \frac{2u^6}{6} + \frac{u^8}{8} + C $$
$$ = \frac{\sin^4(x)}{4} - \frac{\sin^6(x)}{3} + \frac{\sin^8(x)}{8} + C $$

4. When Sine is Odd

Case 2 ($n$ is odd)

  1. Move one $\sin(x)$ next to $dx$: $$ \int \sin^{n-1}(x)\cos^m(x)\sin(x) \, dx $$
  2. Rewrite $\sin^{n-1}(x)$ in terms of $\cos(x)$ using: $$ \sin^2(x) = 1 - \cos^2(x) $$
  3. Make the $u$-sub   $\mathbf{u = \cos(x)}$   to find the antiderivative.
Example ($n=3, m=2$)
$$ \int \sin^3(x)\cos^2(x) \, dx = \int \sin^2(x)\cos^2(x)\sin(x) \, dx $$ $$ = \int (1 - \cos^2(x))\cos^2(x)\sin(x) \, dx $$
$u = \cos(x)$
$du = -\sin(x) \, dx$
$$ = -\int (1 - u^2)u^2 \, du $$ $$ = -\int (u^2 - u^4) \, du $$ $$ = -\left( \frac{u^3}{3} - \frac{u^5}{5} \right) + C $$
$$ = -\left( \frac{\cos^3(x)}{3} - \frac{\cos^5(x)}{5} \right) + C $$
