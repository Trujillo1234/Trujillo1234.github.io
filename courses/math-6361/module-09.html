Module 9

The Logic of Quantifiers

Variables, quantifiers, scope, first-order translation, and the move from propositional to predicate logic.

Guiding Questions

  • How does first-order logic extend propositional logic?
  • What is the relationship between an "expression," a "well-formed formula," and a "sentence"?
  • How can you tell if a well-formed formula is a sentence?
  • What makes a universally quantified sentence true?
  • What makes an existentially quantified sentence true?
  • What is the "domain of discourse"?
  • What sentences are logical truths?
  • What arguments involving quantification are valid?
  • When are two wffs logically equivalent?
  • What are De Morgan's laws for quantifiers?

Objectives

  • Recognize and construct wffs in a first-order language.
  • Identify if a variable is free or bound in a wff.
  • Recognize if a wff is an atomic wff.
  • Recognize if a wff is a sentence.
  • Determine if a quantified sentence is true or false.
  • Identify the truth-functional form of a sentence with quantifiers.
  • Determine if a quantified sentence is a tautology.
  • Determine if a quantified sentence is a tautological consequence of some collection of sentences.
