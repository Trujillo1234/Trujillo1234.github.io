About

I am a mathematician whose work moves between Ramsey theory, ultrafilters, foundations of mathematics, set theory, mathematical logic, and the design of courses where students are asked to do serious mathematical thinking out loud. My research began in topology and analysis and eventually found its way into topological Ramsey spaces and the Tukey theory of ultrafilters. My teaching has moved through nearly the whole undergraduate mathematics curriculum, from algebra and trigonometry through calculus, differential equations, proof, algebraic structures, and logic.

This site is being rebuilt as a durable academic home. The current version keeps the structure deliberately clear: a cover page, a real CV, a teaching page informed by my teaching award materials, and staging pages for research and past courses. The course and research archives will return one piece at a time, with the original mathematical structure respected instead of flattened into summaries.

Short Biography

I received my B.S. in mathematics from New Mexico Tech, my M.S. in mathematics from New Mexico Tech, and my Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Denver. Before joining Sam Houston State University in 2017, I taught at New Mexico Tech, the University of Denver, Texas State University, and the Colorado School of Mines.
