Calculus 2 • The Interval of Convergence
Objective
Find the interval of convergence of a power series.

1. The Interval of Convergence

Power Series

A power series centered at $x=a$ is given by: $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (x-a)^n $$
Q: What is the domain of $f(x)$?
A: All values of $x$ where the series converges.

Center of Convergence: $x=a$

$$ f(a) = a_0 + a_1 \cdot 0 + a_2 \cdot 0 + a_3 \cdot 0 + \dots = a_0 $$

Radius of Convergence ($R$)

Visualizing the interval on a number line:

div    ?   [abs. conv.]   ?    div
<---(|)//////////////////(|)--->
  a-R         a         a+R

2. Finding the Radius $R$

Calculate the limit $L$ using the Ratio or Root test: $$ \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right|=L \quad \text{or} \quad \lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt[n]{|a_n|}=L $$
  • 1. $L=0 \longrightarrow R=\infty \longrightarrow$ Interval: $(-\infty, \infty)$
  • 2. $L=\infty \longrightarrow R=0 \longrightarrow$ Interval: $[a,a]$
  • 3. $0 < L < \infty \longrightarrow R=1/L$
Check Endpoints (Four Outcomes): $$ (a-R, a+R) $$ $$ [a-R, a+R) $$ $$ (a-R, a+R] $$ $$ [a-R, a+R] $$

3. Example 1

Ex: Find the interval of convergence of $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n}{n^2}(x-10)^n $$
Center: $a=10$
Coefficient: $a_n = \dfrac{10^n}{n^2}$
Ratio Test: $$ \begin{aligned} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{10^{n+1}}{(n+1)^2} \cdot \frac{n^2}{10^n} \right| &= \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{10n^2}{(n+1)^2} \right| \\ &= \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{10n^2}{(n+1)^2} \\ &\overset{L.H.}{=} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{20n}{2(n+1)} \\ &\overset{L.H.}{=} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{20}{2} = 10 \end{aligned} $$ $$ \implies L=10 \implies R = \frac{1}{10} $$

Endpoints:

  • $x = a + R = 10 + 1/10 = 10.1$
  • $x = a - R = 10 - 1/10 = 9.9$
When $x=10.1$: $$ \begin{aligned} f(10.1) &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n}{n^2}(10.1 - 10)^n \\ &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n}{n^2}\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^n \\ &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} \end{aligned} $$ This is a p-series with $p=2$. By p-series test, it converges.

When $x=9.9$: $$ \begin{aligned} f(9.9) &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n}{n^2}(9.9 - 10)^n \\ &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{10^n}{n^2}\left(-\frac{1}{10}\right)^n \\ &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2} \end{aligned} $$ Alt. Series Test with $b_n = \frac{1}{n^2}$:
  1. $b_n > 0$ for all $n \ge 1$.
  2. For all $n \ge 1$, $b_{n+1} = \frac{1}{(n+1)^2} \le \frac{1}{n^2} = b_n$.
  3. $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = 0$.
Since (a), (b), and (c) are all true, the alt. series test shows that $\sum \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2}$ converges.
Interval of convergence: $[9.9, 10.1]$

4. Example 2

Ex: Find the interval of convergence of $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n+1} $$
Center: $a=0$
Coefficient: $a_n = \dfrac{1}{n+1}$
Ratio Test: $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{1}{n+2} \cdot \frac{n+1}{1} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{n+1}{n+2} \overset{L.H.}{=} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{1} = 1 $$ Radius of conv. $R=1$.

Endpoints:

  • $x = a + R = 0 + 1 = 1$
  • $x = a - R = 0 - 1 = -1$
When $x=1$: $$ f(1) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1^n}{n+1} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n+1} $$ Compare to the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n}$, a p-series with $p=1$. Since $p \le 1$, it diverges.
Using Limit Comparison Test: $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{\frac{1}{n+1}}{\frac{1}{n}} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{n}{n+1} \overset{L.H.}{=} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{1} = 1. $$ By the limit comp. test, $\sum \frac{1}{n+1}$ must also diverge.

When $x=-1$: $$ f(-1) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n+1} $$ Alt. Series Test with $b_n = \frac{1}{n+1}$:
  1. For all $n \ge 1$, $b_n > 0$.
  2. For all $n \ge 1$, $b_{n+1} = \frac{1}{n+2} \le \frac{1}{n+1} = b_n$.
  3. $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n+1} = 0$.
Since (a), (b), and (c) are all true, the alt. series test implies that $\sum \frac{(-1)^n}{n+1}$ converges.

The interval of conv. includes $x=-1$ and excludes $x=1$.

Interval of conv: $[-1, 1)$
