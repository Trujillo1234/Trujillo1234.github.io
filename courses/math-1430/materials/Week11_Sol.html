1. $\int x^2 \sin(x) \, dx$

$u$ $dv$
$+$ $x^2$ $\sin(x)$
$-$ $2x$ $-\cos(x)$
$+$ $2$ $-\sin(x)$

$$ = -x^2 \cos(x) + 2x \sin(x) + 2 \cos(x) + C $$

3. $\int x^2 \ln(x) \, dx$
$u = \ln(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = x^2 \, dx$
$v = \frac{x^3}{3}$
$$ = \frac{x^3}{3} \ln(x) - \int \frac{x^3}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$ $$ = \frac{x^3}{3} \ln(x) - \frac{1}{3} \int x^2 \, dx $$ $$ = \frac{x^3}{3} \ln(x) - \frac{1}{3} \left( \frac{x^3}{3} \right) + C $$
$$ = \frac{x^3}{3} \ln(x) - \frac{x^3}{9} + C $$

7. $\int (\ln(x))^2 \, dx$
$u = (\ln(x))^2$
$du = 2\ln(x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = dx$
$v = x$
$$ = x(\ln(x))^2 - \int x \cdot 2\ln(x)\cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$ $$ = x(\ln(x))^2 - 2 \int \ln(x) \, dx $$

Now apply integration by parts again for $\int \ln(x) \, dx$:

$u = \ln(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = dx$
$v = x$
$$ = x(\ln(x))^2 - 2 \left( x\ln(x) - \int x \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx \right) $$ $$ = x(\ln(x))^2 - 2x\ln(x) + 2 \int 1 \, dx $$
$$ = x(\ln(x))^2 - 2x\ln(x) + 2x + C $$
8. $\int 2\pi x (\ln(x))^2 \, dx$
$u = (\ln(x))^2$
$du = 2\ln(x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = 2\pi x \, dx$
$v = \pi x^2$
$$ = \pi x^2(\ln(x))^2 - \int \pi x^2 \cdot 2\ln(x)\cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx $$ $$ = \pi x^2(\ln(x))^2 - 2\pi \int x\ln(x) \, dx $$

Now apply integration by parts for $\int x\ln(x) \, dx$:

$u = \ln(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$
$dv = x \, dx$
$v = \frac{x^2}{2}$
$$ = \pi x^2(\ln(x))^2 - 2\pi \left( \frac{x^2}{2}\ln(x) - \int \frac{x^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx \right) $$ $$ = \pi x^2(\ln(x))^2 - \pi x^2\ln(x) + \pi \int x \, dx $$
$$ = \pi x^2(\ln(x))^2 - \pi x^2\ln(x) + \frac{\pi x^2}{2} + C $$

11. $I = \int e^{2x} \sin(3x) \, dx$
$u = \sin(3x)$
$du = 3\cos(3x) \, dx$
$dv = e^{2x} \, dx$
$v = \frac{e^{2x}}{2}$
$$ I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \int \frac{e^{2x}}{2} \cdot 3\cos(3x) \, dx $$ $$ I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3}{2} \int e^{2x} \cos(3x) \, dx $$

Apply integration by parts again:

$u = \cos(3x)$
$du = -3\sin(3x) \, dx$
$dv = e^{2x} \, dx$
$v = \frac{e^{2x}}{2}$
$$ I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3}{2} \left[ \frac{\cos(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \int \frac{e^{2x}}{2} (-3\sin(3x)) \, dx \right] $$ $$ I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3\cos(3x)e^{2x}}{4} - \frac{9}{4} \int e^{2x}\sin(3x) \, dx $$ $$ I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3\cos(3x)e^{2x}}{4} - \frac{9}{4} I $$ $$ \frac{13}{4} I = \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3\cos(3x)e^{2x}}{4} $$
$$ I = \frac{4}{13} \left( \frac{\sin(3x)e^{2x}}{2} - \frac{3\cos(3x)e^{2x}}{4} \right) + C $$
12. $I = \int 5e^{-x} \cos(x) \, dx$
$u = \cos(x)$
$du = -\sin(x) \, dx$
$dv = 5e^{-x} \, dx$
$v = -5e^{-x}$
$$ I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) - \int (-5e^{-x})(-\sin(x)) \, dx $$ $$ I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) - 5\int e^{-x}\sin(x) \, dx $$

Apply integration by parts again:

$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$dv = e^{-x} \, dx$
$v = -e^{-x}$
$$ I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) - 5\left[ -e^{-x}\sin(x) - \int -e^{-x}\cos(x) \, dx \right] $$ $$ I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) + 5e^{-x}\sin(x) - \int 5e^{-x}\cos(x) \, dx $$ $$ I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) + 5e^{-x}\sin(x) - I $$ $$ 2I = -5e^{-x}\cos(x) + 5e^{-x}\sin(x) $$
$$ I = \frac{1}{2}\left( -5e^{-x}\cos(x) + 5e^{-x}\sin(x) \right) + C $$
13. $\int 5 \tan^{-1}(3x) \, dx$
$u = \tan^{-1}(3x)$
$du = \frac{3}{1+9x^2} \, dx$
$dv = 5 \, dx$
$v = 5x$
$$ = 5x \tan^{-1}(3x) - \int \frac{15x}{1+9x^2} \, dx $$

Now use $u$-substitution: $u = 1+9x^2 \Rightarrow du = 18x \, dx \Rightarrow \frac{1}{18} du = x \, dx$

$$ = 5x \tan^{-1}(3x) - \frac{15}{18} \int \frac{1}{u} \, du $$ $$ = 5x \tan^{-1}(3x) - \frac{15}{18} \ln|u| + C $$
$$ = 5x \tan^{-1}(3x) - \frac{15}{18} \ln|1+9x^2| + C $$
14. $\int 7 \cos^{-1}(x) \, dx$
$u = \cos^{-1}(x)$
$du = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx$
$dv = 7 \, dx$
$v = 7x$
$$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) - \int 7x \left( \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \right) dx $$ $$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) + \int \frac{7x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx $$

Use $u$-substitution: $u = 1-x^2 \Rightarrow du = -2x \, dx \Rightarrow -\frac{1}{2} du = x \, dx$

$$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) - \frac{7}{2} \int u^{-1/2} \, du $$ $$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) - \frac{7}{2} \left( \frac{u^{1/2}}{1/2} \right) + C $$ $$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) - 7 \sqrt{u} + C $$
$$ = 7x \cos^{-1}(x) - 7\sqrt{1-x^2} + C $$
15. $I = \int 5^x \sin(x) \, dx$
$u = \sin(x)$
$du = \cos(x) \, dx$
$dv = 5^x \, dx$
$v = \frac{5^x}{\ln(5)}$
$$ I = \frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \int \frac{5^x}{\ln(5)} \cos(x) \, dx $$

Apply integration by parts again:

$u = \cos(x)$
$du = -\sin(x) \, dx$
$dv = 5^x \, dx$
$v = \frac{5^x}{\ln(5)}$
$$ I = \frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \frac{1}{\ln(5)} \left[ \frac{\cos(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \int \frac{5^x}{\ln(5)} (-\sin(x)) \, dx \right] $$ $$ I = \frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \frac{\cos(x)5^x}{(\ln(5))^2} - \frac{1}{(\ln(5))^2} \int 5^x \sin(x) \, dx $$ $$ I = \frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \frac{\cos(x)5^x}{(\ln(5))^2} - \frac{1}{(\ln(5))^2} I $$ $$ I \left( 1 + \frac{1}{(\ln(5))^2} \right) = \frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \frac{\cos(x)5^x}{(\ln(5))^2} $$
$$ I = \frac{\frac{\sin(x)5^x}{\ln(5)} - \frac{\cos(x)5^x}{(\ln(5))^2}}{1 + \frac{1}{(\ln(5))^2}} + C $$

19. $\int (5 \sin^{-1}(x) + 7 \cos^{-1}(x)) \, dx$

Break this into two integrals. From problem (14) we showed:

$$ \int 7\cos^{-1}(x) \, dx = 7x\cos^{-1}(x) - 7\sqrt{1-x^2} + C $$

Now find $\int 5\sin^{-1}(x) \, dx$:

$u = \sin^{-1}(x)$
$du = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx$
$dv = 5 \, dx$
$v = 5x$
$$ = 5x\sin^{-1}(x) - \int \frac{5x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx $$

Use $u$-substitution: $u = 1-x^2 \Rightarrow du = -2x \, dx \Rightarrow -\frac{1}{2} du = x \, dx$

$$ = 5x\sin^{-1}(x) + \frac{5}{2} \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{u}} \, du = 5x\sin^{-1}(x) + 5\sqrt{u} $$ $$ = 5x\sin^{-1}(x) + 5\sqrt{1-x^2} $$

Thus, summing both results:

$$ = 7x\cos^{-1}(x) - 7\sqrt{1-x^2} + 5x\sin^{-1}(x) + 5\sqrt{1-x^2} + C $$
20. $\int (4x^3 - 3x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx$

Integrate term by term using the Power Rule:

$$ = \frac{4x^4}{4} - \frac{3x^3}{3} + \frac{2x^2}{2} - x + C $$
$$ = x^4 - x^3 + x^2 - x + C $$
21. $\int (x + 2)(x - 3) \, dx$

First, expand the polynomial:

$$ = \int (x^2 - 3x + 2x - 6) \, dx = \int (x^2 - x - 6) \, dx $$

Now integrate using the Power Rule:

$$ = \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{x^2}{2} - 6x + C $$

24. $\int (\sec(x)\tan(x) + \csc^2(x)) \, dx$

Recognize the standard fundamental trigonometric antiderivatives:

$$ \int \sec(x)\tan(x) \, dx = \sec(x) $$ $$ \int \csc^2(x) \, dx = -\cot(x) $$
$$ = \sec(x) - \cot(x) + C $$
25. $\int (2^x + e^x) \, dx$

Use the basic exponential integral rules:

$$ \int a^x \, dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln(a)} \quad \text{and} \quad \int e^x \, dx = e^x $$
$$ = \frac{2^x}{\ln(2)} + e^x + C $$
