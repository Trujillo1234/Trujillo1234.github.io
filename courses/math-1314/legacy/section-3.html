Section 3 Functions

Section Context

This unit shifts the course from solving isolated equations to thinking about functions as mathematical objects. Students move among formulas, graphs, tables, and verbal descriptions while learning domain, range, rate of change, composition, transformations, absolute value functions, and inverse functions.

  • Learning objectives: evaluate function notation, find domains and ranges, calculate average rates of change, compose functions, transform graphs, and verify inverse relationships.
  • Representative example: for \(f(x)=\sqrt{x-3}\), explain why the domain is \([3,\infty)\) before evaluating or graphing the function.
  • Connection forward: function structure drives the polynomial, rational, exponential, and logarithmic models that close the course.
