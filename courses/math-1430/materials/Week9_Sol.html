1. Area of a Region

Find the area of each shaded region.

  1. Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Area} &= \int_{\pi/6}^{5\pi/6} \left(\sin(x) - \frac{1}{2}\right) dx \\ &= \left[ -\cos(x) - \frac{1}{2}x \right]_{\pi/6}^{5\pi/6} \\ &= \left( -\cos\left(\frac{5\pi}{6}\right) - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{5\pi}{6} \right) - \left( -\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right) - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\pi}{6} \right) \\ &= \left( -\left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) - \frac{5\pi}{12} \right) - \left( -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{\pi}{12} \right) \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{5\pi}{12} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} + \frac{\pi}{12} \\ &= \sqrt{3} - \frac{4\pi}{12} \\ &= \sqrt{3} - \frac{\pi}{3} \end{align*} $$
  2. Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Area} &= \int_{1}^{3} \left( (-x^2 + 4x) - 3 \right) dx \\ &= \int_{1}^{3} \left( -x^2 + 4x - 3 \right) dx \\ &= \left[ -\frac{x^3}{3} + 2x^2 - 3x \right]_{1}^{3} \\ &= \left( -\frac{(3)^3}{3} + 2(3)^2 - 3(3) \right) - \left( -\frac{(1)^3}{3} + 2(1)^2 - 3(1) \right) \\ &= \left( -\frac{27}{3} + 18 - 9 \right) - \left( -\frac{1}{3} + 2 - 3 \right) \\ &= \left( -9 + 18 - 9 \right) - \left( -\frac{1}{3} - 1 \right) \\ &= 0 - \left( -\frac{4}{3} \right) \\ &= \frac{4}{3} \end{align*} $$
  3. Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Area} &= \int_{0}^{2\pi} (\sin(x) + 1) dx \\ &= \left[ -\cos(x) + x \right]_{0}^{2\pi} \\ &= \left( -\cos(2\pi) + 2\pi \right) - \left( -\cos(0) + 0 \right) \\ &= (-1 + 2\pi) - (-1) \\ &= -1 + 2\pi + 1 \\ &= 2\pi \end{align*} $$

2. Volume by Rotation about the $x$-Axis

Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating each shaded region about the $x$-axis.

  1. Solution

    Using the disk method:

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{1}^{4} \pi \left( \sqrt{\frac{\ln(x)}{x}} \right)^2 - \pi(0)^2 dx \\ &= \int_{1}^{4} \pi \cdot \frac{\ln(x)}{x} dx = \pi \int_{1}^{4} \ln(x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} dx \end{align*} $$

    Let $u = \ln(x)$, then $du = \frac{1}{x} dx$. The new limits of integration are $u(1) = \ln(1) = 0$ and $u(4) = \ln(4)$.

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \pi \int_{0}^{\ln(4)} u \, du \\ &= \pi \left[ \frac{u^2}{2} \right]_{0}^{\ln(4)} \\ &= \pi \left( \frac{(\ln(4))^2}{2} - 0 \right) \\ &= \frac{\pi (\ln(4))^2}{2} \end{align*} $$
  2. Solution

    Using the washer method:

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{0}^{1} \pi (x^2+1)^2 - \pi (x)^2 dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{1} \pi (x^4 + 2x^2 + 1) - \pi x^2 dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{1} \left( \pi x^4 + \pi x^2 + \pi \right) dx \\ &= \left[ \pi \frac{x^5}{5} + \pi \frac{x^3}{3} + \pi x \right]_{0}^{1} \\ &= \left( \pi \cdot \frac{1^5}{5} + \pi \cdot \frac{1^3}{3} + \pi \cdot 1 \right) - (0) \\ &= \pi \left( \frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{3} + 1 \right) \\ &= \pi \left( \frac{3}{15} + \frac{5}{15} + \frac{15}{15} \right) \\ &= \frac{23\pi}{15} \end{align*} $$
  3. Solution

    Using the disk method:

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{0}^{4} \pi \left(\sqrt{x}\right)^2 - \pi(0)^2 dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{4} \pi x \, dx \\ &= \left[ \pi \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_{0}^{4} \\ &= \pi \frac{(4)^2}{2} - \pi \frac{(0)^2}{2} \\ &= 8\pi \end{align*} $$

3. Volume by Rotation about the $y$-Axis

Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating each shaded region about the $y$-axis.

  1. Solution

    Using the shell method:

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{0}^{2} 2\pi x ((-x+3) - 0) dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{2} 2\pi x (-x+3) dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{2} 2\pi (-x^2 + 3x) dx \\ &= 2\pi \left[ -\frac{x^3}{3} + 3\frac{x^2}{2} \right]_{0}^{2} \\ &= 2\pi \left( \left(-\frac{2^3}{3} + 3 \frac{2^2}{2}\right) - (0) \right) \\ &= 2\pi \left( -\frac{8}{3} + \frac{12}{2} \right) \\ &= 2\pi \left( -\frac{8}{3} + 6 \right) \\ &= 2\pi \left( \frac{-8}{3} + \frac{18}{3} \right) \\ &= \frac{20\pi}{3} \end{align*} $$
  2. Solution

    First, find the intersection point in the first quadrant:

    $$ \begin{align*} 1 &= \sqrt{4-x^2} \\ 1 &= 4-x^2 \\ x^2 &= 3 \implies x = \sqrt{3} \end{align*} $$

    Using the shell method:

    $$ \text{Vol} = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} 2\pi x \left( \sqrt{4-x^2} - 1 \right) dx = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \pi \left( \sqrt{4-x^2} - 1 \right) 2x \, dx $$

    Let $u = 4-x^2$, then $du = -2x dx \implies -du = 2x dx$. The limits become $u(0) = 4 - 0^2 = 4$ and $u(\sqrt{3}) = 4 - (\sqrt{3})^2 = 1$.

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{4}^{1} \pi (\sqrt{u} - 1) (-du) \\ &= -\int_{4}^{1} \pi (\sqrt{u} - 1) du \\ &= -\pi \left[ \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} - u \right]_{4}^{1} \\ &= -\pi \left( \frac{1^{3/2}}{3/2} - 1 \right) + \pi \left( \frac{4^{3/2}}{3/2} - 4 \right) \\ &= -\pi \left( \frac{2}{3} - 1 \right) + \pi \left( \frac{8}{3/2} - 4 \right) \\ &= -\pi \left( -\frac{1}{3} \right) + \pi \left( \frac{16}{3} - \frac{12}{3} \right) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{3} + \pi \left( \frac{4}{3} \right) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{3} + \frac{4\pi}{3} \\ &= \frac{5\pi}{3} \end{align*} $$
  3. Solution

    Using the shell method:

    $$ \begin{align*} \text{Vol} &= \int_{1}^{3} 2\pi x \left( \frac{1}{x} - 0 \right) dx \\ &= \int_{1}^{3} 2\pi x \cdot \frac{1}{x} dx \\ &= \int_{1}^{3} 2\pi \, dx \\ &= \left[ 2\pi x \right]_{1}^{3} \\ &= 2\pi(3) - 2\pi(1) \\ &= 6\pi - 2\pi \\ &= 4\pi \end{align*} $$

4. Surface Area of a Surface of Revolution

Set up an integral (but do not evaluate) that represents the surface area of the curve rotated about the given axis.

  1. $y=\sqrt{x}$ on $[1,4]$, rotated about the $x$-axis.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} \\ \text{Surface Area} &= \int_{1}^{4} 2\pi \sqrt{x} \sqrt{1 + \left( \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} \right)^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  2. $y=x^2$ on $[1,3]$, rotated about the $y$-axis.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= 2x \\ \text{Surface Area} &= \int_{1}^{3} 2\pi x \sqrt{1 + (2x)^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  3. $y=\sqrt[3]{3x}$ on $[1,2]$, rotated about the $y$-axis.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \frac{1}{3}(3x)^{-2/3} \cdot 3 = (3x)^{-2/3} \\ \text{Surface Area} &= \int_{1}^{2} 2\pi x \sqrt{1 + \left( (3x)^{-2/3} \right)^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  4. $y=\ln(x^2+x)$ on $[1,10]$, rotated about the $x$-axis.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x} \\ \text{Surface Area} &= \int_{1}^{10} 2\pi \ln(x^2+x) \sqrt{1 + \left( \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x} \right)^2} dx \end{align*} $$

5. Arc Length

Set up an integral (but do not evaluate) for the length of the curve on the given interval.

  1. $y=\sin(x)$ on $[0,2\pi]$.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \cos(x) \\ \text{Length} &= \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{1 + (\cos(x))^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  2. $y=\dfrac{1}{1+5x}$ on $[1,2]$.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= -(1+5x)^{-2} \cdot 5 = \frac{-5}{(1+5x)^2} \\ \text{Length} &= \int_{1}^{2} \sqrt{1 + \left( \frac{-5}{(1+5x)^2} \right)^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  3. $y=5^x$ on $[5,10]$.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \ln(5) 5^x \\ \text{Length} &= \int_{5}^{10} \sqrt{1 + (\ln(5) 5^x)^2} dx \end{align*} $$
  4. $y=\ln|\cos(x)|$ on $[0,\pi/4]$.
    Solution
    $$ \begin{align*} y' &= \frac{-\sin(x)}{\cos(x)} = -\tan(x) \\ \text{Length} &= \int_{0}^{\pi/4} \sqrt{1 + (-\tan(x))^2} dx \end{align*} $$
