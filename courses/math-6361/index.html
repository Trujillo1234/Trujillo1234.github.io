Mathematical Logic

This archive is adapted from the Blackboard course modules. It keeps the shape of the course while removing private course logistics, due dates, quiz links, and submission instructions. Each module collects guiding questions, objectives, reading and video notes, activities, and homework practice.

Module 0

Atomic Sentences

Names, predicates, functions, atomic formulas, and the first pieces of formal language.

Module 1

Logic of Atomic Sentences

Arguments in the blocks language, validity, consequence, and the way atomic statements support formal reasoning.

Module 2

Boolean Connectives

Negation, conjunction, disjunction, and the syntax used to build compound sentences.

Module 3

Logic of Boolean Connectives

Truth tables, logical equivalence, tautologies, contradictions, and validity for Boolean forms.

Module 4

Formal Proof Methods for Boolean Logic

Fitch-style proof, introduction and elimination rules, and proof strategies for propositional logic.

Module 5

Logic of Conditionals

Conditionals, biconditionals, truth-functional analysis, and custom connectives.

Module 6

Soundness & Completeness

The relationship between proof and truth, soundness, completeness, and what a deductive system promises.

Module 7

Midterm Review

Review of propositional language, semantic methods, formal proof, and core proof strategies.

Module 9

The Logic of Quantifiers

Variables, quantifiers, scope, first-order translation, and the move from propositional to predicate logic.

Module 10

Proof Methods for Quantifiers

Informal and formal methods for universal and existential reasoning.

Module 11

Formal Proof Methods for Quantifiers

Formal quantifier rules, restrictions on variables, and structured first-order derivations.

Module 12

Axiomatic Set Theory

First-order axioms, set-theoretic language, separation, and mathematical structures.

Modules 13/14

Completeness and Incompleteness

Deductive systems for first-order logic, completeness, incompleteness, and the limits of formal systems.
