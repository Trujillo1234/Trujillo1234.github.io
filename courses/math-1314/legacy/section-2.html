Section 2 Equations and Inequalities

Section Context

This unit rebuilds the algebra students need for later function work: coordinate geometry, linear equations, modeling, complex numbers, quadratic equations, higher-degree equations, and inequalities. The embedded videos are used as worked examples and pacing support for the main course path.

  • Learning objectives: plot and interpret points, solve linear and quadratic equations, identify complex solutions, and express inequalities with interval notation.
  • Representative example: solve \(|x-2|<5\), graph the solution set, and explain the interval notation \((-3,7)\) in words.
  • Connection forward: equations become tools for finding intercepts, domains, inverse functions, and model parameters in later sections.
