Module 4

Formal Proof Methods for Boolean Logic

Fitch-style proof, introduction and elimination rules, and proof strategies for propositional logic.

Guiding Questions

  • What does the reasoning pattern of conjunction introduction look like?
  • What does the reasoning pattern of conjunction elimination look like?
  • What does the reasoning pattern of disjunction introduction look like?
  • What kind of formula, as a premise, is the sign of applying proof by cases?
  • Are we able to tell which case(s) is in fact the case by the end of our proof?
  • What is the formal name of the fancy proof method reductio ad absurdum ?
  • What does it mean when we say that a set of sentences is contradictory?
  • What follows from inconsistent premises?
  • Are arguments from inconsistent premises valid?
  • Are arguments from inconsistent premises sound?

Objectives

  • Construct formal proofs using the Fitch program.
  • Formalize informal proofs by contradiction.
  • Formalize informal proofs that use the method of cases.
  • Recognize and apply elimination and introduction rules for conjunction, disjunction, negation, and contradiction.
  • Properly use subproofs when constructing formal proofs by hand in the Fitch format.
  • Recall that arguments with inconsistent premises are always valid but never sound.
