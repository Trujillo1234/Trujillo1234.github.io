Area and Volume

Learning Objectives
  • Find the area of a region of type 1.
  • Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating a region of type 1 about the x-axis. Method of disks/washers
  • Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating a region of type 1 about the y-axis. Method of cylindrical shells

1. Type 1 Regions

Type 1 Region on the Interval $[a,b]$

A type 1 region is a region bounded above by $y=f(x)$ and below by $y=g(x)$ for $a \le x \le b$.

Top curve: $y=f(x)$  •  Bottom curve: $y=g(x)$

2. How to Find the Area of a Type 1 Region

Partition Idea

Break the interval $[a,b]$ into subintervals of width

$$ \Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n}. $$
Each slice spans from the lower curve to the upper curve.
One rectangle has height $f(x_i)-g(x_i)$ and width $\Delta x$.
$$ A_i = \big(f(x_i)-g(x_i)\big)\Delta x $$ $$ \text{total area} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} A_i $$ $$ = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \big(f(x_i)-g(x_i)\big)\Delta x $$ $$ = \int_a^b \big(f(x)-g(x)\big)\,dx $$

3. Area Examples

Example 1: Find the area of the region.

The region is bounded by $y=\sin(x)$ and $y=\cos(x)$ from $x=0$ to $x=\pi/4$.

cos(x) lies above sin(x) on $[0, \pi/4]$
$$ \text{area} = \int_0^{\pi/4} \cos(x)-\sin(x)\,dx $$ $$ = \sin(x)+\cos(x)\Big|_0^{\pi/4} $$ $$ = \big(\sin(\pi/4)+\cos(\pi/4)\big)-\big(\sin(0)+\cos(0)\big) $$ $$ = \sqrt{2}-1 $$
Example 2: Find the area of the region.

The region is bounded by $y=e^x+1$ and $y=x+1$ on the interval $[0,2]$.

$e^x+1$ lies above $x+1$ on $[0, 2]$
$$ \text{area} = \int_0^2 \big((e^x+1) - (x+1)\big)\,dx $$ $$ = \int_0^2 e^x-x\,dx $$ $$ = e^x - \frac{x^2}{2} \Big|_0^2 $$ $$ = \big(e^2-2\big)-\big(e^0-0\big) $$ $$ = e^2-3 $$

4. Volume About the x-axis

Disk/Washer Method

When a type 1 region is rotated about the x-axis, the cross-sections are washers.

$$ \text{Total volume about the x-axis} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \pi\Big((f(x_i))^2-(g(x_i))^2\Big)\Delta x $$ $$ = \int_a^b \pi\Big((f(x))^2-(g(x))^2\Big)\,dx $$
One washer (face-on)
Area of face $= \pi(f(x_i))^2 - \pi(g(x_i))^2$
Stack of washers along x-axis
Washers are butted against each other along the x-axis;
$V_i = \pi\big((f(x_i))^2-(g(x_i))^2\big)\Delta x$

5. Volume About the y-axis

Cylindrical Shells

For rotation about the y-axis, use a vertical slice to form a cylindrical shell.

A vertical strip at $x_i$ becomes a shell when rotated about the y-axis.
5 nested shells butted against each other around the y-axis (n=5 approximation).
$$ V_i = 2\pi x_i\big(f(x_i)-g(x_i)\big)\Delta x $$ $$ \text{Total volume about the y-axis} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} 2\pi x_i\big(f(x_i)-g(x_i)\big)\Delta x $$ $$ = \int_a^b 2\pi x\big(f(x)-g(x)\big)\,dx $$
Summary: Volume Methods
Rotate about the y-axis
$$ \int_a^b 2\pi x\big(f(x)-g(x)\big)\,dx $$
(Shells)
Original region
$$ \int_a^b \big(f(x)-g(x)\big)\,dx $$
Rotate about the x-axis
$$ \int_a^b \pi\Big((f(x))^2-(g(x))^2\Big)\,dx $$
(Disk/Washer)

6. Volume Examples

Example 1: Find the volume obtained by rotating the region about the x-axis.

The region is under $y=\sqrt{\sin(x)}$ from $x=0$ to $x=\pi$.

Solid of revolution about the x-axis
$$ \text{volume} = \int_0^{\pi} \pi\big(\sqrt{\sin(x)}\big)^2 - \pi(0)^2\,dx $$ $$ = \int_0^{\pi} \pi\sin(x)\,dx $$ $$ = -\pi\cos(x)\Big|_0^{\pi} $$ $$ = -\pi\cos(\pi) + \pi\cos(0) $$ $$ = 2\pi $$
Example 2: Find the volume when rotating about the y-axis.

The region is under $y=\sin(x^2)$ from $x=0$ to $x=\sqrt{\pi}$.

Flat torus (solid of revolution about the y-axis)
$$ \text{volume} = \int_0^{\sqrt{\pi}} 2\pi x\sin(x^2)\,dx $$
U-Substitution
$u=x^2$
$du=2x\,dx$
$u(0)=0^2=0$
$u(\sqrt{\pi})=(\sqrt{\pi})^2=\pi$
$$ = \int_0^{\pi} \pi\sin(u)\,du $$ $$ = -\pi\cos(u)\Big|_0^{\pi} $$ $$ = -\pi\cos(\pi) + \pi\cos(0) $$ $$ = 2\pi $$
