Mathematics

My research is in Ramsey theory, topological Ramsey spaces, ultrafilters, nonstandard models of arithmetic, mathematical logic, and foundations of mathematics. Much of my work studies how large combinatorial and logical structures can be organized, classified, and understood through the behavior of ultrafilters and related Ramsey-theoretic objects.

My initial mathematical training was in analysis and topology, and my doctoral work led me into topological Ramsey spaces, associated ultrafilters, Tukey theory, and Dedekind cuts of nonstandard models of arithmetic. I remain interested in the way combinatorial structure, logical form, and infinite objects interact.

Teaching

I teach across the undergraduate and graduate mathematics curriculum. My teaching is connected to the same general concern that shapes my research: mathematical ideas need structure, language, and public reasons. Students should learn not only to reach correct answers, but to explain why the steps are valid in a form that someone else can read, check, and build on.

Background

I received my B.S. in mathematics from New Mexico Tech, my M.S. in mathematics from New Mexico Tech, and my Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Denver. Before joining Sam Houston State University in 2017, I taught at New Mexico Tech, the University of Denver, Texas State University, and the Colorado School of Mines.
