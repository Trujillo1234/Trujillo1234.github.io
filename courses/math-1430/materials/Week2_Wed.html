Radius of Convergence

Calculus 2 Power Series Instructor Notes
Learning Objectives
  • Apply term-by-term integration and differentiation to find the derivative and antiderivative of a power series.
  • Find the Center and Radius of Convergence of a power series.

1. Definition and Domain

Definition: Power Series A power series centered at $x=a$ is defined as: $$ f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (x-a)^n $$ $$ = a_0 + a_1(x-a) + a_2(x-a)^2 + \dots $$ $$ = \lim_{k \to \infty} (a_0 + a_1(x-a) + \dots + a_k(x-a)^k) $$

The Big Question: When does this limit exist? (i.e., What is the domain of $f(x)$?)

Observation #1: $f(a) = a_0 + 0 + 0 + \dots = a_0$.
That is, $x=a$ is always in the domain of $f$.

Observation #2: If $x=b$ is in the domain of $f(x)$, and $a \leq c \leq b$, then $x=c$ is also in the domain.

Take Home Message The domain of $f(x)$ centered at $x=a$ is an interval.

The value $R$ is called the radius of convergence of $f(x)$.

2. Background: Why Geometric Series?

Before finding $R$, we need to understand the theoretical basis. Power series convergence tests are essentially comparisons to the Geometric Series.

Geometric Series Convergence

Theorem: The geometric series $\sum x^n$ converges to $\frac{1}{1-x}$ if and only if $|x| < 1$.

Proof Sketch:
Let $S_n = 1 + x + \dots + x^n$.
Then $S_n - xS_n = 1 - x^{n+1}$.
Solving for $S_n$: $$ S_n = \frac{1 - x^{n+1}}{1 - x} $$ Taking the limit as $n \to \infty$: If $|x| < 1$, then $x^{n+1} \to 0$ and the sum converges.

3. Finding the Radius & Center

To find the radius, we use methods that measure how closely the power series resembles a geometric series.

Two Methods
Method 1: Ratio Test $$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| = L $$
Method 2: Root Test $$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|} = L $$

Three Possible Cases:

  1. If $L=0$, the domain is all real numbers ($R = \infty$).
  2. If $L=\infty$, the domain is only $x=a$ ($R = 0$).
  3. If $0 < L < \infty$, the domain is a finite interval ($R = 1/L$).
Why do we care?
Term-by-term integration and differentiation preserve the center and radius of convergence. (Behavior at endpoints may change).

4. Examples

Example 1 (Finite Radius) Find the center and radius of: $$ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^n}{5^n} $$

Identify: $a_n = \frac{(-1)^n}{5^n}$ and Center $a=0$.

Ratio Test:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{5^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{5^n}{(-1)^n} \right| = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| -\frac{1}{5} \right| = \frac{1}{5} $$

Result: $L = 1/5$, so $R = \frac{1}{1/5} = 5$.

Example 2 (Infinite Radius) $$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(x-3)^n}{n!} $$

Identify: $a_n = \frac{1}{n!}$, Center $a=3$.

Ratio Test:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{1}{(n+1)!} \cdot \frac{n!}{1} \right| = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n+1} = 0 $$

Result: $L=0$, so $R = \infty$.

Note: $(n+1)! = (n+1)n!$. Root test is hard to evaluate here.

Example 3 $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n(x-3)^n}{7^n} $$

Identify: $a_n = \frac{n}{7^n}$, Center $a=3$.

Ratio Test:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{n+1}{7^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{7^n}{n} \right| = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left( \frac{n+1}{n} \cdot \frac{1}{7} \right) = \frac{1}{7} $$

Result: $R = 7$.

Example 4 (Root Test) $$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 8^n (x-3)^n $$

Identify: $a_n = 8^n$, Center $a=3$.

Root Test:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|8^n|} = 8 $$

Result: $R = 1/8$.

Example 5 (Zero Radius) $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n! (x+3)^n $$

Identify: $a_n = n!$, Center $a=-3$.

Ratio Test:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{(n+1)!}{n!} \right| = \lim_{n \to \infty} (n+1) = \infty $$

Result: $R = 0$.

5. Calculus on Power Series

Problem: Find the integral and derivative of each power series.

Consider the functions:

  • $f(x) = \sum n! x^{2n}$
  • $g(x) = \sum \frac{x^n}{5^n}$
  • $h(x) = \sum (-1)^n x^{5n}$
  • $k(x) = \sum \frac{(x-3)^n}{(2n)!}$
Derivatives $$ f'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 2n \cdot n! x^{2n-1} $$ $$ g'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n x^{n-1}}{5^n} $$ $$ h'(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 5n (-1)^n x^{5n-1} $$ $$ k'(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n(x-3)^{n-1}}{(2n)!} $$
Integrals $$ \int f(x) dx = C + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n! x^{2n+1}}{2n+1} $$ $$ \int g(x) dx = C + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n+1}}{5^n(n+1)} $$ $$ \int h(x) dx = C + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{5n+1}}{5n+1} $$ $$ \int k(x) dx = C + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(x-3)^{n+1}}{(2n)!(n+1)} $$

Algebraic Manipulation: We can multiply/divide series by $x$.

Examples

1. $ \displaystyle \frac{e^x}{x} = \frac{1}{x} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n-1}}{n!} $

2. $ \displaystyle x \sin(x) = x \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+2}}{(2n+1)!} $
