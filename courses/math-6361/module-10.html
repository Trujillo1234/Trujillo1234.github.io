Module 10

Proof Methods for Quantifiers

Informal and formal methods for universal and existential reasoning.

Guiding Questions

  • What sentences are logical truths?
  • What arguments involving quantification are valid?
  • Does changing the order of quantification in a sentence change the meaning of a sentence?
  • Are function symbols necessary?
  • What are the valid inference patterns for involving quantifiers?
  • What does existential introduction look like in a proof?
  • What does a general conditional proof look like?
  • What is the Barber Paradox?
  • What does universal introduction look like in a proof?

Objectives

  • Use the central vocabulary and methods from proof methods for quantifiers to read, translate, evaluate, and explain logical arguments.
