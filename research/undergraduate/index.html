Undergraduate and Student Research

I think student research works best when students are given a real mathematical object to handle. That object might be a graph, a voting rule, a numerical method, a modal logic, a bridge model, or a strange generalized trigonometric system. The project becomes serious when students begin making definitions, testing examples, finding limits, and writing in a way that lets someone else check the work.
